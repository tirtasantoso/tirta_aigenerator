# Godric's Stats Journal Example

**Year 12,000 — The Incident of Absolute Speed**
**Penned by Arch-Mage Vane, 8th Court Wizard of Thronegate**

The handwriting in this journal was precise, academic, and utterly devoid of emotion, the script of a man who had dissociated so completely from the horror he had witnessed that his pen could only record the clinical facts. The ink was a faded, rusty brown, and the parchment crackled like frozen leaves as I turned the pages.

*I am writing this account three hours after the conclusion of the Chronal Incursion. My hands are not trembling, though I suspect this is only because my nervous system has entered a state of profound, protective shock from which it may never fully emerge. I must record what I witnessed before the memory degrades, though I suspect the memory will outlast my physical body and persist, screaming, into whatever afterlife awaits the servants of the apex predator.*

*The Chrono-Reapers were not a species in any conventional sense. They were hyper-dimensional assassins who had evolved beyond linear biology to become living weapons of temporal erasure. Their bodies were composed of solidified negative time, and they moved backward through the causal stream with a fluidity that made the concept of "speed" meaningless. They did not dodge attacks. They simply ensured that the attacks had never been launched, sliding backward to the moment before the hostile intent was formed and erasing the attacker from the timeline with surgical precision. They had unmade seventeen thousand civilizations in this manner, each one erased so completely that not even the memory of their existence remained in the cosmic record.*

*When Thronegate's envoys arrived to deliver the offer of unconditional surrender, the Chrono-Reapers did not negotiate. They simply reached backward through the timeline and erased the envoys' grandparents from ever having been born. Our diplomats flickered out of existence, their entire bloodlines retroactively annihilated, leaving behind only a faint, aching void in the memories of those who had known them.*

*Godric received the report in the throne room. He was sitting perfectly motionless upon the obsidian dais, His ruggedly handsome face illuminated by the amber glow of the arcane sconces. His short salt-and-pepper hair did not stir. His heavily muscled frame, draped in simple dark garments, remained absolutely still. He required no breath, no sustenance, no sleep. He was an immortal monolith of apocalyptic power, and He had been sitting in that exact position for three days, simply existing, because existence was all He needed to do.*

*I delivered the report myself, my voice steady despite the cold dread coiling in my gut. Godric listened without expression. His absolute omniscience had already perceived the erasure of the envoys, had already traced the temporal manipulation backward to its source, had already catalogued every atom of the Chrono-Reapers' existence. He knew everything. He simply nodded once and rose from the throne.*

*He materialized in the Chrono-Reapers' temporal nexus, a vast, churning maelstrom of inverted causality that existed outside the boundaries of linear time. The Reapers, numbering in the conceptual decillions, surrounded Him instantly, their negative-time bodies flickering across every possible moment of attack simultaneously. Their High Executor, a towering vortex of solidified temporal negation, broadcast a declaration directly into the fabric of reality. It declared that the Chrono-Reapers could not be outsped, outmaneuvered, or outlasted. They existed at every point in time simultaneously, and they would erase Godric from the timeline before His first heartbeat had ever occurred.*

*Godric did not activate His magic. He did not summon Gent or deploy any of His infinite passive shields. He withheld exactly ninety-nine point nine-nine percent of His true power, continuing the nines infinitely into a recursion of self-negation so profound that the value of His restraint could not be mathematically expressed. He wanted to test a single stat. His Speed.*

*I must now attempt to describe what I witnessed through my astral scrying matrix, though the act of writing these words causes the blood vessels in my eyes to throb with sympathetic rupture. Godric's Speed, like all His attributes, has been multiplying infinitely by itself every single Planck second for twelve thousand years. It is not a measure of how fast He can move. It is a fundamental, absolute constant of His existence that determines the maximum possible velocity at which any being in any reality can traverse any distance, and it has long since surpassed every theoretical limit that mathematics can encode. The fraction of Speed that He allowed to express itself was so infinitesimally, incomprehensibly small that calling it zero percent would be a laughable, grotesque overestimation. If you divided a Planck second by infinity, and then divided that result by infinity again, and continued this division recursively for the entire lifespan of the omniverse, you would still not approach a number small enough to accurately represent the fraction of His Speed that He permitted Himself to use.*

*He did not move His body. He did not take a step. He did not lift His hand. He merely decided to shift His gaze to look at them.*

*The microscopic, fractional velocity of His irises rotating in their sockets generated a temporal friction so catastrophically immense that the fourth dimension itself shattered like cheap glass. He looked at the Chrono-Reapers faster than the concept of time could process the event. The Reapers, existing at every point in time simultaneously, were instantly caught in the kinetic slipstream of His shifting gaze. Their negative-time bodies, which had never been touched, never been struck, never been perceived by any enemy in their entire history, were violently, excruciatingly smeared across fifty billion distinct historical epochs simultaneously.*

*The agony was absolute. The Chrono-Reapers did not die. Death would have been a mercy that His Speed denied them. Instead, their conceptual bodies were stretched infinitely thin across the entire span of cosmic history, their nervous systems elongated into screaming, conscious threads that experienced every single moment of their simultaneous dismemberment eternally. Their minds, designed to process temporal information with perfect clarity, were flooded with fifty billion epochs of sensory input simultaneously—the heat death of universes, the birth of stars, the screams of dying civilizations, the silence of the void—all crashing into their consciousness at once, an unending, inescapable torrent of absolute, mind-shattering sensory overload.*

*But the collateral damage of His eye movement was obscenely, incomprehensibly catastrophic. The temporal friction generated by His shifting gaze ignited the entire chronal plane. The foundational fabric of time across thousands of entire multiverses was instantly, violently combusted. Trillions of timelines, each one containing countless civilizations, countless histories, countless living beings with families and dreams and children, were retroactively deleted from existence in a fraction of a millisecond. The screaming of the Chrono-Reapers was drowned out by the silent, absolute erasure of more sentient life than I can ever hope to quantify.*

*Godric, His gaze now settled on the empty space where the Chrono-Reapers had been, registered the sudden, glaring emptiness of the cosmic background. His expression flickered with the faintest shadow of mild annoyance, the look of a man who had accidentally knocked over a glass of water. He sighed, a soft, barely audible exhalation, and then, effortlessly, without moving a single muscle, without spending even the faintest glimmer of His true power, He restored the thousands of incinerated multiverses in exactly zero seconds. Every decillion of decillion beings was instantly resurrected, their timelines perfectly reconstructed, their memories flawlessly intact. They would never know they had been erased. They would never know that their existence was permitted solely by the whimsical mercy of an apex predator who had simply decided they should return.*

*He did not restore the Chrono-Reapers. They remain an eternal, agonizing smear of bloody static on the perimeter of time, their stretched, screaming consciousnesses stretched across fifty billion epochs of unending, inescapable torment. Their vocal cords, such as they were, continue to emit a sound that will never be heard by anyone, a continuous, wet shriek of absolute, mind-shattering agony that will persist until the last star in the omniverse gutters out and dies.*

*I closed my scrying matrix and vomited onto the obsidian floor. My hands are still shaking. I have served my King faithfully for forty years, and I will continue to serve Him until my mortal body fails. But I will never, ever forget the sound that the chronal plane made when it ignited. It sounded like a child screaming.*
