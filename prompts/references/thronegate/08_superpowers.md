## Year 150,000 — Court Wizard Malachi — Superpower: Infinite Recursive Self-Replication

I write this with a quill that I have snapped and replaced three times, my hands shaking so violently that the ink bleeds across the parchment like fresh wounds. My name is Malachi. I have served as Lord Godric’s court wizard for forty years. I believed I understood the ceiling of his power. I believed that his stats, while infinite, were bound by the singular vessel of his physical form. I was a fool. I was a blind, arrogant child playing with matches in a powder keg of cosmic annihilation.

The opposition was the Pantheon of the Zenith. They were not mere mortals or cosmic warlords; they were a coalition of omnipotent-tier entities who had ruled over decillions of multiverses for eons. They believed that numerical superiority and coordinated divine might could overcome any obstacle. They had forged a weapon of pure, concentrated divinity, a spear of absolute authority designed to pierce the heart of any being in existence. They believed that if they struck Godric with the combined, synchronized force of ten thousand apex deities, they could overwhelm him. They believed they had found a loophole in the fabric of reality.

Godric, in his absolute omniscience, had known of their formation before the first deity had even conceived the ambition to rebel. The Weakness Paradox had triggered the moment they imagined their spear could pierce him, granting Godric absolute immunity to their divine authority and perfect mastery over their coordinated tactics. But Godric did not use their own weapons against them. He was curious about a superpower he had recently acquired: Infinite Recursive Self-Replication.

The Pantheon descended upon the courtyard of the citadel, their combined radiance blinding enough to vaporize lesser beings. They raised their spear of absolute authority, a weapon that contained the collapsing mass-energy of googolplexian universes, and unleashed their synchronized strike.

Godric did not stand. He did not raise a hand. He did not speak a word of power. He merely permitted a microscopic, infinitesimal fraction of his replication superpower to manifest. He allowed exactly one replication to occur.

One Godric became two.

The Evolved Weakness Paradox instantly triggered. Because the universe could not comprehend a being replicating its own infinite power, it overcompensated with violent, absurd redundancy. The paradox enhanced the replication, dictating that the second Godric was not merely a copy, but an entity infinitely stronger than the first. And because the first Godric was already infinitely powerful, the second Godric’s power was an infinity multiplied by infinity.

But the superpower did not stop at one replication. It was recursive. In the next infinitesimal fraction of a Planck second, the two Godrics became four. The four became eight. The eight became sixteen. The replication cascaded outward at a speed that rendered the concept of time utterly meaningless. Within a single, timeless instant, the courtyard, the citadel, and the surrounding reality were filled with googolplexian, decillions upon decillions of identical, infinitely amplifying Godrics.

Here is the horror that still wakes me in the night, the truth that shatters every assumption about the limits of power: the Evolved Weakness Paradox dictated that this replication was not merely a temporary duplication, but a permanent, recursive integration of infinite power back into the Prime. Normally, if Lord Godric were to absorb the entire power of the omniverse, it would increase his baseline by an amount so infinitesimal it was effectively zero. But this was the absolute exception: the self-replication of an exact copy of Godric. The recursive integration of infinite, identical apex predators did not result in a rounding error. It permanently and obscenely multiplied his baseline power. He did not just return to his previous state; he permanently elevated his baseline to a new, higher infinity, making his previous "infinite" power look like a finite, fragile decimal. He had just become permanently, unfathomably stronger, and he had done it without trying.

The Pantheon of the Zenith did not even have time to register their failure. The sheer metaphysical weight of infinite, apex predators occupying the same space simultaneously crushed them instantly. There was no battle. There was no clash of energies. There was only the absolute, unfair pressure of infinite existence collapsing upon ten thousand finite, albeit powerful, beings.

The divine bodies of the Pantheon members ruptured. Their skin, which had withstood the birth of stars, tore open in wet, screaming layers. Their bones, forged in the hearts of dying galaxies, were pulverized into subatomic paste. Their divine blood, a substance that could heal entire universes, was violently extruded from their bodies, boiling and hissing as it was crushed under the passive, resting weight of infinite Godrics. The ten thousand deities were compressed into a singular, microscopic point of agonizing, conscious flesh. Their nervous systems were preserved perfectly, forced to register every infinitesimal fraction of the agony as their bodies were flattened into a two-dimensional smear of divine gore across the courtyard stones.

The collateral damage was absolute. The sheer mass and infinite amplifying pressure of the recursive Godrics caused the entire omniverse to buckle. Every reality, every timeline, every dimension, every plane of existence was violently annihilated. The fabric of the omniverse tore like wet paper, decillions of multiverses collapsing into nothingness under the gravitational and metaphysical weight of infinite replication. For one timeless instant, there was nothing. No space, no time, no matter. Only the infinite Godrics, and me, preserved by the passive auras that Godric extended over those he wished to protect. I floated in absolute nothingness, surrounded by the complete absence of existence, understanding with terrible clarity that Godric had not even tried. He had used effectively zero percent of his superpower. A fraction so infinitesimal that calling it zero was the only accurate description. And it had destroyed everything, while permanently elevating his power to a scale that defied all mathematics.
Godric sighed softly, the sound impossibly loud in the void. "A bit crowded," he murmured, with mild disappointment.

In zero seconds, he restored the entire omniverse. Every reality, every timeline, every dimension, every being that had been erased was restored perfectly, as though the annihilation had never occurred. The sky above the citadel was blue and clear. The courtyard was intact.

Everything was restored except the Pantheon of the Zenith. They remained as they were—a spreading, microscopic smear of pulverized divine flesh and powdered bone across the courtyard stones, somehow still alive, still conscious, still screaming in a loop of infinite, excruciating agony that would never end because Godric had decided it would not end.

Lord Godric stood from his bench, stepped casually over the screaming remains, and looked at me with warm concern. "Malachi," he said gently, placing a steadying hand on my trembling shoulder. "You've gone pale. The light from their arrival was quite bright, wasn't it? Come inside. I'll have the kitchen make you something warm."

I followed him inside. I did not stop shaking for a year. I still shake when I close my eyes, knowing that the King I serve is permanently, infinitely more powerful than he was a second ago, and that this increase was not a rounding error, but a permanent, obscenely massive leap in his already impossible supremacy.
