Year 210,400 — Court Wizard Elias — Superpower: Infinite Recursive Apex Self-Replication

I write this with a quill that I have snapped and replaced seven times, my hands shaking so violently that the ink bleeds across the parchment like the fresh wounds of slaughtered gods. My name is Elias. I have served as Lord Godric’s court wizard for forty years. I believed I understood the absolute ceiling of his power. I believed that his stats, while infinite, were bound by the singular vessel of his physical form. I was a fool. I was a blind, arrogant child playing with matches in a powder keg of cosmic annihilation. Yesterday, I watched Lord Godric utilize a superpower that permanently, obscenely altered the baseline mathematics of his own existence, and the visceral gore of the resulting massacre will haunt my bloodline forever.

The opposition was the Meta-Pantheon of the Zenith Void. They were a coalition of decillions of omnipotent-tier pantheons, each individual pantheon containing countless apex deities who had ruled over the conceptual frameworks of existence for eons. They believed that absolute numerical superiority and coordinated divine mass could overcome any singular obstacle. They had forged a conceptual spear of absolute density designed to violently crush the physical form of any being in existence. They believed that if they struck Godric with the combined, synchronized force of decillions of apex deities, they could overwhelm him. They believed they had found a loophole in the fabric of reality, a mathematical blind spot where infinite power could be beaten by sheer numerical volume.

Godric, in his absolute omniscience, had known of their formation before the first deity had even conceived the ambition to rebel. The Weakness Paradox had triggered the moment they imagined their spear could crush him, granting Godric absolute immunity to their physical density and perfect mastery over their coordinated tactics. But Godric did not use their own weapons against them. He was curious about a superpower he had recently acquired: Infinite Recursive Self-Replication.

The Meta-Pantheon descended upon the grand hall of the citadel, their massive, galaxy-spanning forms compressed into localized avatars of pure, blinding physical density that caused the surrounding space-time to actively hemorrhage. They raised their spear of absolute mass, a weapon that contained the collapsing mass-energy of countless universes, and unleashed their synchronized, omniversal strike.

Godric did not stand. He did not raise a hand. He did not speak a word of power. He merely permitted an infinitesimal fraction of his replication superpower to manifest at exactly zero percent of his true effort.

In absolute zero time, instantly exceeding the very concept of chronological progression, one Godric became an infinite number of Godrics.

The Evolved Weakness Paradox instantly triggered. Because the universe could absolutely not comprehend a being replicating its own infinite power, it overcompensated with violent, absurd redundancy. Every single replicated Godric was a perfect, identical Prime. Each copy possessed the exact same absolute omniscience, the exact same infinitely amplifying stats, and the exact same arsenal of apocalyptic superpowers. However, the paradox aggressively dictated that each newly generated replica be infinitely magnified beyond the original, creating a permanent, localized feedback loop of ascending omnipotence that completely shattered all mathematical frameworks.

Within a single, timeless absolute zero instant, every conceivable dimension, every microscopic space between atoms, every timeline, and every plane of existence across the entire omniverse was violently occupied by an infinite expanse of identical, exponentially magnifying Godrics.

Here is the absolute horror that still wakes me in the night, the truth that violently shatters every assumption about the limits of power: the Evolved Weakness Paradox dictated that this replication was not merely a temporary duplication, but a permanent, recursive integration of infinite power back into the Prime. Normally, if Lord Godric were to absorb the entire power of the omniverse, it would increase his baseline by an amount so infinitesimal it was effectively zero. But this was the absolute exception: the self-replication of identical copies of Godric. The recursive integration of infinite identical apex predators did not result in a rounding error. It permanently and obscenely multiplied his baseline power. He did not just return to his previous state; he permanently elevated his baseline to a new, higher infinity, making his previous infinite power look like a finite, fragile decimal. He had just become permanently, unfathomably stronger, and he had done it without trying.

The Meta-Pantheon of the Zenith Void did not even have time to register their absolute failure. The sheer ontological density of infinite, identical, exponentially magnified apex predators simultaneously occupying the exact same reality completely obliterated them. It was not a matter of mere physical weight crushing them; it was absolute spatial and metaphysical displacement. There was no battle. There was only the aggressive, unfair dimensional pressure of infinite expanding existences violently overwriting the finite space the Pantheon occupied.

The physical and conceptual bodies of the decillions of Pantheon members aggressively ruptured. Their hyper-dense divine skin violently peeled apart into wet, screaming layers of flayed meat. Their bones, forged in the crushing gravity of black holes, were instantaneously pulverized into a fine, subatomic paste by the sheer metaphysical footprint of the King. Their divine blood, a substance that possessed the mass of dying galaxies, aggressively boiled and hissed as it was violently extruded from their pulverized pores under the crushing spatial pressure of infinite Godrics. The decillions of deities were conceptually compressed into a singular, microscopic point of agonizing, conscious flesh. Their divine nervous systems were preserved perfectly by the paradox, forcefully keeping them conscious to register every infinitesimal fraction of the absolute agony as their bodies were flattened into a two-dimensional smear of divine gore across the marble floor of the grand hall.

The collateral damage was absolute. The infinite expanding presence of the recursive Godrics instantaneously caused the entire omniverse to violently buckle and shatter infinite times over. Every reality, every timeline, every dimension, every plane of existence was aggressively annihilated by the sheer dimensional displacement of Godric’s replication. The fabric of the omniverse tore like wet tissue paper, decillions of multiverses violently collapsing into absolute nothingness under the ontological footprint of infinite Godrics. For one timeless zero-second instant, there was nothing. No space, no time, no matter. Only the infinite Godrics, and me, preserved by the passive auras that Godric extended over those he wished to protect.

Godric sighed softly, the sound impossibly loud in the absolute void. "A bit crowded," he murmured, with mild disappointment.

In zero seconds, he effortlessly restored the entire omniverse. Every reality, every timeline, every dimension, every being that had been erased was restored perfectly, as though the annihilation had never occurred. The sky above the citadel was blue and clear. The grand hall was perfectly intact.

Everything was restored except the Meta-Pantheon. They remained exactly as they were—a spreading, microscopic smear of pulverized divine flesh, shattered organs, and powdered bone across the marble stones, somehow still alive, still entirely conscious, still screaming in a loop of infinite, excruciating physical agony that would never end because Godric had decided it would not end.

Lord Godric stood, his physical form now back to a singular, permanently elevated entity, stepped casually over the violently screaming remains, and looked at me with warm concern. "Elias," he said gently, placing a steadying hand on my violently trembling shoulder. "You've gone pale. The sudden crowd must have startled you. Come inside. I'll have the kitchen make you something warm."

I followed him inside. I did not stop violently shaking for an entire year. I still shake when I close my eyes, knowing that the King I serve is permanently, infinitely more powerful than he was a second ago, and that this increase was not a rounding error, but a permanent, obscenely massive leap in his already impossible physical supremacy.

I dropped Elias’s journal onto the low wooden table. The sound echoed in the quiet study like a hammer violently striking an anvil. Infinite recursive replication in zero seconds. Identical copies, infinitely magnified, generating an ontological displacement so severe it overwrote reality itself. The decillions of gods crushed into a two-dimensional bloody smear by spatial pressure alone. The permanent baseline increase. My own thousandfold daily amplification felt incredibly pathetic, a cruel joke played by the universe, like a single weak grain of sand trying to comprehend the sheer, crushing mass of a boundless desert. The existential dread Elias had described was violently blossoming in my own chest, suffocating my lungs. I reached for the final journal, my fingers completely numb and slick with cold sweat.
