The vault of the Royal Court Wizards is buried precisely forty miles beneath the continental crust of the imperial capital. It is a suffocating, lightless sphere forged from hyper-compressed neutronium, designed to withstand the ambient, accidental arcane friction that my bloodline passively generates. My name is Kaedan. I am twenty-two years old. It is the Year One Million of the eternal empire of Thronegate. For exactly two years, my physical strength, my kinetic speed, my cognitive processing, and my raw magical capacity have multiplied by a factor of one thousand every single morning. I am a mortal man, yet my circulatory system is a pressurized containment vessel for apocalyptic arcane energy.
My father, Malakor, the previous Court Wizard, escorted me down into this lightless tomb four hours ago. This is the Rite of Witnessing. Before we are allowed to stand beside the throne, we must completely, utterly, and violently shatter our own pride. We must read the primary source accounts of our predecessors to understand the absolute, inescapable futility of our existence, and the terrifying, mind-bending reality of the apex predator we serve.
I sat at the obsidian desk. Resting upon its cold surface were five heavy, leather-bound journals. I opened the oldest tome, its pages brittle and yellowed, written in the cramped, academic script of Arch-Mage Vane, who served during the Year Twelve Thousand. Vane wrote of the Chrono-Reapers, hyper-dimensional assassins who moved backward through negative time. They invaded the throne room, freezing the chronal flow of the universe to an absolute, dead halt, intending to decouple Godric from the timeline.
Vane’s ink was dark and heavy. He noted that Godric—an immortal entity completely devoid of biological necessities, requiring absolutely no oxygen, sustenance, or sleep—was sitting perfectly motionless on his throne. Godric did not activate his magic. He withheld exactly ninety-nine point nine-nine percent, continuing the nines infinitely, of his true power. He wanted to test a single stat: his **Speed**.
I traced my glowing violet finger over Vane’s frantic, sterile words. *The King did not move his body. He merely decided to shift his gaze to look at them. Because his Speed multiplies infinitely by itself every Planck second, the microscopic, fractional velocity of his irises shifting generated a temporal friction so absolute it shredded the fourth dimension. He looked at them faster than the concept of time itself could process. The Chrono-Reapers were instantly caught in the kinetic slipstream of his shifting gaze. Their conceptual bodies were violently smeared across fifty billion distinct historical epochs simultaneously, their nervous systems stretched infinitely thin, leaving them permanently conscious and screaming in unending temporal dismemberment. But the collateral damage of his eye movement was obscenely catastrophic. The friction ignited the chronal plane. Thousands of entire multiverses surrounding our reality were retroactively deleted from existence, their trillions of timelines burning to ash in a fraction of a millisecond. The King, slightly annoyed by the sudden, glaring emptiness of the cosmic background, effortlessly, in exactly zero seconds, restored the thousands of incinerated multiverses, resurrecting countless decillion beings with their memories flawlessly intact. He did not restore the Chrono-Reapers. They remain an eternal, agonizing smear of bloody static on the perimeter of time.*
My hands trembled as I turned the pages. I closed Vane’s journal and pulled the second book toward me. It was bound in scaled leather, written by Court Wizard Silas in the Year Forty-Five Thousand. Silas possessed a precise, militaristic, and chillingly analytical voice.
Silas wrote of an alliance of hyper-gods from the furthest reaches of the omniverse. They had spent millennia collapsing an entire, fully populated multiverse down into a single, microscopic needle of pure, ontological mass. It was a weapon of absolute, crushing density, designed to pierce the King's skin. They launched it directly at Godric’s cheek at speeds transcending logic.
Silas’s meticulous handwriting broke down into jagged, terrified strokes. *The King possessed absolute omniscience. He knew the needle was coming. He deliberately ordered his sentient passive aura, Gent, to stand down. He deactivated his infinite layers of passive shields. He remained perfectly motionless, withholding ninety-nine point nine-nine percent of his power, allowing only a fraction of his **Durability** to manifest. The impact broke the foundation of mathematics. The needle, possessing the raw mass of an entire multiverse, struck his cheek. The King's Durability, having amplified infinitely every Planck second for millennia, possessed a density that rendered the concept of physical matter obsolete. The needle did not pierce him. It did not leave a microscopic scratch. It shattered upon his skin. But because his flesh refused to yield even a fraction of a millimeter, the kinetic energy violently inverted. The resulting rebound shockwave obliterated the attacking hyper-gods, atomizing their immortal forms instantly. The shockwave did not stop. It tore outward, violently unspooling the physical laws of the local omniversal quadrant. One hundred million surrounding universes violently imploded, collapsing in on themselves under the crushing, rebounding force of the King’s unyielding skin. The King, completely unbothered, sighed at the mess. Without exerting a single ounce of effort, he instantly reconstructed the one hundred million imploded universes perfectly. The hyper-gods remained erased, denied any resurrection.*
I pushed Silas’s journal away, gasping for air. My amplified heart pounded against my ribs like a hammer. I reached for the third tome, a heavy book bound in white silk, written by High Wizard Kaelen in the Year Two Hundred and Fifty Thousand. Kaelen’s voice was pious, dramatic, and stained with a profound, weeping terror.
Kaelen chronicled the invasion of the Devouring Swarm. They were a hive-mind of biological horrors, trillions of emotionless, chitinous beasts that blotted out the skies of a million galaxies, driven solely by a cold imperative to consume. They were immune to fear and pain.
Godric, Kaelen wrote, sat upon his throne, utterly still. He found their chattering noise irritating. He completely suppressed his strength, his speed, and his magic, holding back an infinite expanse of zeroes. He allowed only the most microscopic fraction of his **Charisma** to leak through his absolute concealment.
Kaelen’s prose devolved into frantic poetry. *The King’s Charisma is a weaponized, conceptual dominance over the biological imperative of affection. The fraction of Charisma that washed over the emotionless Devouring Swarm violently overwrote their foundational biology. The hive-mind’s cold logic was instantly, brutally replaced by a sudden, mind-shattering, absolute devotion. Trillions of monstrous beasts hung suspended in the atmosphere of a million galaxies, violently forced to comprehend the flawless, infinite majesty of his existence. They experienced a worship so absolute, a love so terrifyingly intense, that their own continued existence felt like a grotesque insult to his perfect beauty. Driven completely insane by the sheer weight of his Charisma, massive, continent-sized leviathans tore their own hearts from their chests. Trillions of beasts shredded their own flesh with their talons, bathing a million galaxies in a torrential downpour of their own steaming viscera, committing mass, species-wide suicide. The collateral damage was sickening. The sheer psychic shockwave of their fanatical, hysterical self-mutilation generated a wave of sympathetic grief so intense that the stars in a billion adjacent galaxies instantly went supernova, detonating in a synchronized apocalypse of conceptual sorrow. The King watched them tear themselves apart with a warm, friendly smile. He flawlessly restored the billion detonated galaxies in zero seconds. He left the Swarm to rot in their own blood.*
A cold sweat slicked my skin. I pulled the fourth journal toward me, a thin, erratic volume written by Royal Mage Thaddeus in the Year Seven Hundred Thousand. The handwriting was jagged, frantic, and deeply paranoid.
Thaddeus documented the Whisper Cult, an abstract coalition of reality-warpers who physically relocated their entire civilization into the abstract, mathematical void between dimensions. They existed in the silence between thoughts, intending to launch a conceptual strike that bypassed all physical reality.
Godric did not cast a spell of unmaking. He withheld all offensive capabilities. He simply allowed a microscopic fraction of his **Senses**—specifically his Hearing—to passively unfold.
*The King’s Hearing,* Thaddeus scribbled in jagged ink, *multiplies infinitely. He simply listened to them. The sheer, catastrophic "mass" of his auditory focus collapsing onto their abstract dimension was too heavy for their reality to sustain. The act of being listened to by the King violently collapsed their conceptual waveforms. The Cult was brutally, agonizingly dragged from the abstract plane directly into physical, screaming reality within the throne room. The weight of his attention forced their abstract concepts to rapidly condense into biological matter, manifesting as writhing, screaming masses of raw, bleeding brain tissue and exposed nerves. But the collateral damage of the King passively "listening" was an omniversal catastrophe. The infinite sensitivity of his hearing inadvertently amplified the ambient background noise of the cosmos. The foundational silence of the omniverse violently shattered. The eardrums and sanity of every single living entity across a billion multiverses ruptured simultaneously in a deafening, reality-breaking screech. The King effortlessly, instantly reset the biology of the billions of multiverses, healing their ruptured minds and ears. The Cult, however, remained as a pulsing mound of screaming auditory meat on his floor, drowning in their own exposed fluids.*
I was trembling violently. The sheer, relentless horror was suffocating. I reached for the final book. It was the journal of my father, Malakor, written exactly two years ago. The ink was dark, the handwriting exhausted, weary, and distinctly paternal.
My father wrote of the Colossi of the Outer Dark, hyper-brawlers forged in the crushing gravity wells of collapsing multiverses. Each Colossus possessed the physical mass of a galaxy compressed into a thirty-foot-tall humanoid frame. They breached the throne room, relying entirely on raw, unadulterated physical strength, boasting they possessed the kinetic force to shatter reality.
My father described how Godric, appearing as the warm, friendly, forty-five-year-old uncle I had known my entire life, simply sat on the obsidian throne. He did not move. He withheld ninety-nine point nine-nine percent, extending infinitely, of his physical might. He allowed only an infinitesimally microscopic fraction of his raw **Strength** to manifest.
*The King did not punch them,* my father wrote, the ink smearing where his hand had clearly trembled. *He did not even lift his hand. He merely twitched a single, microscopic muscle fiber in his right pinky finger. The kinetic energy generated by that single muscle fiber contracting instantly deleted the Colossi. The beings possessing the compressed mass of galaxies were instantly, effortlessly vaporized into a fine, sub-atomic red mist, liquefying faster than their synapses could register pain. But the obscenely massive collateral damage could not be contained by the dimension. The kinetic shockwave of that single twitch violently unspooled the physical laws of our reality. The wave traveled outward, instantly pulverizing thousands of entire multiverses into a screaming, chaotic slurry of raw, unshaped plasma. The omniverse itself literally began to crack in half, the foundational pillars of creation snapping under the microscopic exertion of his pinky finger. And then, before the conceptual dust could even settle, the King sighed. Effortlessly, instantly, in exactly zero seconds, he perfectly patched the cracked omniverse, restored the thousands of pulverized multiverses, and re-established the laws of physics. The King simply adjusted his posture on the throne, leaving the sub-atomic red mist of the Colossi floating in the air as a permanent testament to his absolute supremacy.*
The final entry in my father’s journal was addressed directly to me.
*Kaedan. You feel the power burning in your veins. You feel the daily amplification making you a god among mortals. You must understand the absolute truth. We are not his shield. Opposing him is not merely suicide; it is a paradox. It is a mathematical impossibility. Never forget the abyss that hides behind his warm smile. Serve him faithfully, and pray you never give him a reason to test a new stat.*
I closed the journal. The heavy, suffocating silence of the vault pressed against my eardrums. The thousandfold amplification of my magic felt completely, utterly meaningless. I was a speck of dust masquerading as a hurricane.
I stood up from the obsidian desk, my legs shaking, and walked toward the adamantine door. I pressed my palm against the release mechanism, and the heavy door ground open, revealing the brightly lit, pristine corridors of the palace above.
I walked the long, silent path to the imperial throne room. The heavy doors swung open, and I stepped inside.
Godric was sitting perfectly motionless on the throne. He looked exactly as he always did: ruggedly handsome, dressed in a simple, dark tunic. He possessed absolute concealment, projecting no aura, no pressure, requiring no breath, no sustenance, existing as an immortal monolith of apocalyptic power. He looked at me, and his eyes crinkled at the corners as a warm, friendly, entirely genuine smile spread across his face.
"Ah, Kaedan, my boy," Godric said, his rich, comforting baritone echoing in the vast chamber. "You have completed your reading. Excellent. Come, sit. Tell me how your training is progressing."
I looked at the man who considered my bloodline his family. And beneath that genuine warmth, I saw the infinite, inescapable abyss. I saw the shredded timelines, the pulverized multiverses, the screaming meat, and the atomized gods. The horror was absolute. It was unyielding.
"Yes, Uncle Godric," I said, my voice remarkably steady despite the cold, soul-crushing dread permanently anchored in my chest. I walked up the steps of the dais to serve the apex predator of the omniverse, knowing with absolute, terrifying certainty that there was no power in all of creation that could save us if he ever stopped smiling.
