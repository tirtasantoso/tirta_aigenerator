# Hermit God to Capital

## **Core Instruction**

Write a **long, epic, thriller fantasy story**. The entire narrative must be written in **prose paragraphs only**. You are **strictly forbidden** from using **bullet points** or **any LaTeX formatting anywhere in the story**. The story should heavily feature **dialogue, monologue, inner thoughts, and musings** throughout.

## **Narrative Perspective**

The story must be told from the perspective of the **last surviving soldier**, a **36-year-old man named Blake**, who survives only because **the god allows him to live**.

## **Setting**

The story takes place in a fantasy world where an **ancient immortal god named Godric** exists.

Godric must be described as appearing to be in his **mid-30s**, with a **highly masculine, muscular physique**, a **neatly trimmed black-and-pepper beard**, **short hair**, and an **attractively handsome face**. He should be **about the size of the strongest and biggest human soldier**, and **his size must never change anywhere in the story**.

He is ancient beyond comprehension and has lived for **eons** on a **remote island outside human civilization**. Human legend warns people **never to disturb him**.

## **Godric’s Nature and Power**

Throughout the entire story, emphasize that Godric is **holding back constantly**, because he is actually **far stronger, faster, and more intelligent** than the story even shows. Make it clear that he is so overwhelmingly powerful that he **does not even need to move** in order to utterly destroy his opponents.

At the beginning of his conception, all of his attributes, including but not limited to **strength, endurance, speed, intelligence, and every other stat**, began to be multiplied by **1,000 every nanosecond in accelerating fashion**, without exception. The scaling must follow this pattern exactly in implication: the first nanosecond is multiplied by **1,000,000 to the power of 1**, the second by **1,000,000 to the power of 2**, the third by **1,000,000 to the power of 3**, and so on continuously. The story should strongly imply that this process has been ongoing for **eons**.

In addition, Godric gains a **new apocalyptic superpower every second**. Each new superpower is, from the moment it first manifests, already capable of causing the **instant extinction of the entire civilization**, even before any further multiplication. These superpowers are **cumulative**, and every one of them becomes stronger at the **same rate as his attributes every second.**

Godric has **absolutely no weakness**.

He is also **omniscient**.

## **Worldbuilding and Background History**

Include a background history of **past civilizations** that dared to disturb Godric, only to be **completely, instantly, effortlessly, and utterly obliterated by him**. This should reinforce the terror surrounding his existence and the validity of the ancient warning never to disturb his island.

## **Humanity’s Rise**

Show how humanity has evolved to an absurdly powerful peak. Their civilization must be described as possessing all of the following:

The **biggest and most powerful army in the world**.

**Billions of super soldiers**.

The **most technologically advanced civilization in the world**, capable of **conquering galaxies**.

The **strongest magic ever known**.

The **most advanced and deadly robot army**.

**Dragons** powerful enough to **level continents easily**.

**Ninjas** so fast they could **reverse time**.

Show how all of this power made humanity **overconfident**, to the point that they decided to send a **massive army** to Godric’s island in order to **provoke and attack him**.

## **The Attack on the Island**

Show the massive human force arriving at Godric’s island in arrogance and confidence, believing that their unmatched military power, magic, dragons, robots, and impossible warriors are enough to challenge him.

Then show that entire invading force being **instantly and effortlessly decimated by Godric**.

Because of this provocation, Godric retaliates by **teleporting to the human capital**.

## **The Human Capital Confrontation**

In the human capital, show that humanity is still initially confident, because they possess what they believe is the **biggest and strongest army in the world**.

Depict soldiers and forces attacking Godric with **magic, guns, fire, nukes, missiles**, and every other devastating weapon they possess, yet make it clear that **all projectiles and attacks are completely useless against him**. Show that Godric **does not feel any damage at all**.

## **Godric’s Mass Slaughter**

Show Godric wearing a **condescending smirk** as he **casually, nonchalantly, and effortlessly slaughters** an overwhelming human military force, entire cities, and dragons inside a **densely populated nation**.

Describe in great detail just how terrifyingly overpowered he is compared to the army and the entire civilization. Even while displaying this unimaginable power, it must still be obvious that **he is holding back**.

Show that his **mere presence alone** is more than enough to slaughter any humans who dare challenge him, along with the **robot army, ninjas, dragons, helicopters, fighter jets, and tanks**.

Emphasize how overwhelmingly terrifying his **power, reaction time, speed, endurance, and intellect** are in comparison to all of humanity combined.

Show humanity’s confidence slowly collapsing into **complete despair** as they realize they **cannot damage him at all**.

## **The General’s Final Desperation**

Introduce a **male army general** who, in total desperation, orders the use of humanity’s last and most destructive forbidden weapon: the **strongest bombs and nukes ever created**.

Show the deployment of these weapons with maximum dramatic tension, as humanity clings to one final hope.

Then show that these bombs and nukes are **completely useless against Godric**. He must **not feel any damage at all**.

After that, show Godric **nonchalantly and completely decimating the entire remaining army** without exerting any strength or effort whatsoever.

## **Despair and Extinction**

Describe in detail how the entire civilization keeps trying everything it can, giving its absolute best effort, but to no avail. There must be **not even the slightest sign of damage on Godric**, while he casually and effortlessly destroys all of them with overwhelming power.

Show that his **mere presence alone** is enough to utterly decimate anyone who even tries to get close and harm him, and that the **only reason anyone has not already perished** is because **he allows it**.

Show humanity experiencing **true despair** as they witness his absolute power firsthand.

At first, Godric should be **amused** by their struggle.

But after witnessing their repeated futile resistance and the immense losses, humanity eventually **begs him to spare them**.

Godric, however, is **already too bored** by their futile attempts, and he decides to **kill them all**.

## **Ending**

After annihilating humanity, Godric returns to his island.

End the story with a **conclusive final chapter** that ties up the loose ends. The ending should strongly imply that **no one has ever, and no one ever will, make Godric exert any real effort** in order to completely dominate and slaughter them, because he is simply **far too overpowered**.

## **Tone and Style Requirements**

The tone should be **epic, terrifying, oppressive, grand, and thrilling**, with a strong sense of mounting dread and inevitable doom.

Use rich prose and strong atmosphere. Include plenty of **dialogue, monologues, musings, and inner thoughts**, especially through Blake’s perspective, so the reader feels the horror, disbelief, despair, and helplessness of witnessing Godric firsthand.

Do not soften Godric’s superiority. The story must leave no doubt that he is utterly beyond all possible opposition, and that even the most powerful civilization imaginable is nothing to him.
