The version of me that first opened his eyes in the ruined sanctuary—the Year Zero version of me, who had only been amplifying for ten thousand years—could have easily, effortlessly crushed you into a wet smear with a single, passive breath. You are nothing."

Godric did not use a spell. He did not raise his hand. He did not exert a single ounce of effort. He simply, nonchalantly decided that the Creator should cease to exist.

The deconstruction of the First Cause was a display of raw, unmitigated biological and conceptual horror that made my stomach violently heave. Because the Creator was an abstract, infinite being of pure light, Godric’s power violently, aggressively forced him into a physical, biological structure solely so he could experience the physical agony of his own unmaking. 

I watched as the blinding, divine light of God was instantly, grotesquely transmuted into a towering mass of raw, wet, and bleeding human flesh, sprouting trillions of exposed nerve endings, jagged teeth, and weeping, bleeding eyeballs. The Creator’s newly formed, giant bones snapped with a wet, heavy crack as the sheer, physical pressure of Godric’s will began to slowly, methodically grind them into a fine, splintered paste.

The Creator screamed. It was a deafening, unified chorus of absolute, biological agony that vibrated through the vacuum of space. His newly formed skin was violently, cleanly flayed from his muscles, peeling away in thick, bubbling sheets of sizzling grease. His internal organs—lungs made of rotting meat, a heart woven from raw, pulsing nerves—ruptured in a torrential, high-pressure geyser of boiling crimson fluids and yellow grease that painted the ruined courtyard in a thick, dripping coat of divine gore.

The absolute pressure of Godric's will was so ridiculously, catastrophically huge that the entire possible existence—everything that exists, could ever exist, all multiverses, timelines, realities, spaces, and dimensions—was violently, completely annihilated as collateral damage. The fabric of reality was de-atomized and compressed into a hot, screaming sludge of subatomic dust. 

And then, with a casual, non-verbal decision, Godric willed the entire omniverse to restore. In exactly zero seconds, effortlessly and nonchalantly, the entire possible existence was rebuilt, placed exactly where it had been, the memories of the civilians perfectly intact. But the Creator remained permanently, absolutely erased, his divine essence shredded, flayed, and liquefied into a hot, black sludge of divine gore that dissolved forever into the absolute void.

Clive slowly closed the journal, his hands shaking so violently that the flayed-skin binding slipped from his fingers, thudding softly against the floor. He sat perfectly still, his eyes wide and vacant, staring at the dark obsidian table. A cold, thick sweat slicked his skin, smelling sharply of copper and absolute, inescapable terror.

The ultimate realization had settled into his soul like a block of ice. There was no God above Godric. There was no divine justice, no higher authority, no First Cause that could ever step in to save them. The literal Creator of everything—the one who had spun the stars, designed the human body, and written the laws of magic—had been casually, effortlessly forced into a wet, screaming vessel of meat and ground into a bloody sludge because he had dared to stand in the King's path.

Godric was the undisputed, absolute ceiling of all possible existence. His power-scaling was an obscenity, an infinite, recursively compounding engine that grew infinitely stronger with every passing microsecond, leaving the rest of creation in a state of permanent, shivering subjection. Even if every single powerful entity from all possible timelines, all parallel realities, and all infinite permutations of existence were to unite, given unlimited preparation time and boundless resources, they would accomplish absolutely nothing. They could not even make the weakest, most primitive of his passive shields experience the slightest, most microscopic tickle. He could completely, nonchalantly annihilate their combined, desperate mass with zero percent of his power and zero percent of his effort in exactly zero seconds, and that unfathomable, terrifying gap would only continue to accelerate infinitely, recursively, and aggressively forever.

Clive looked toward the door of the solar, his heart slamming against his ribs, knowing that tomorrow he would have to look upon the warm, smiling face of his favorite uncle, and pretend that his soul was not entirely, permanently drowned in a bottomless abyss of absolute, unyielding dread.
