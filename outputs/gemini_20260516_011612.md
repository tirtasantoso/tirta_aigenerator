The chronicle in the 12,000th year of our sovereign’s reign was not the only one I had to read. There were thousands, filed away in the forbidden vaults beneath the high palace, each one a testament to the absolute, suffocating futility of existence. I sat in the dim light of the observatory, the smell of ozone clinging to the pages of the journals like rot, and turned to the record left by the 14th Court Wizard, a man who, like me, was a descendant of the Blake lineage. His journal was written with a frantic, illegible scrawl, the ink splattered with what I realized, with a rising bile in my throat, were the calcified remains of his own tears.

**Year 14,000 — The Incident of Infinite Senses**
**Penned by Court Wizard Kaelen, 14th of Thronegate**

*I had always believed that the King’s omniscience was a static fact of his nature. I was a fool. I did not understand that His senses, like His speed or His strength, are a living, recursive engine of infinite amplification. Today, the Void-Singers of the deep-rift dimensions attempted their final gambit. They were entities of pure sound and vibration, capable of perceiving the internal state of every particle in a galaxy simultaneously. They believed themselves hidden in the eleventh-dimensional shadow-space, a domain theoretically impossible to observe. They spent five thousand years calibrating a resonance frequency specifically designed to harmonize with the King’s nervous system, aiming to drive Him to madness by overloading His consciousness with the collective sensory input of every sentient being in the omniverse.*

*They didn't understand that Godric doesn't just "perceive." He encompasses. When He decided to notice them—a thought that occurred to Him as He was mid-sip of his morning tea—He did not simply turn His gaze toward the shadow-space. He simply allowed the passive, recursive amplification of His senses to express itself for one Planck second. It was effectively zero percent of His awareness. It was a blink, a stray, idle flick of attention.*

*The effect was instantaneous and grotesque. The Void-Singers did not just collapse; they were forcibly converted into literal, pulsating sensory organs. Their consciousness was ripped from their abstract forms and manifested as billions of hyper-sensitive ears, eyes, and nerve endings that stretched across the dimensions, all tethered to the King’s perception. Because His senses were expanding by layered infinities, the sensory overload they experienced was not just the collective input of the omniverse; it was the input of every timeline, every possible future, every atom’s state, and every vibration of reality, past, present, and infinite future, all compressed into their finite, newly forged biological consciousnesses.*

*They screamed. They didn't scream with sound; they screamed with their entire existence, a wave of sheer, unadulterated existential horror that radiated outward and instantly liquefied the physical forms of every living being within a billion-light-year radius. They were forced to perceive Godric’s perception of them. They saw themselves through His omniscient, infinitely amplified vision, and the sheer alienness of being witnessed by a god shattered their souls. They didn't die. They spent what felt like an eternity inside that one Planck second, dying a billion deaths from sensory overstimulation, their bodies turning inside out as their cells tried to process the weight of His awareness. The shockwave of their psychological disintegration leveled three thousand star systems, turning them into clouds of drifting, ionized debris. Godric merely frowned, the look of a man who had heard a slightly annoying hum in the distance, and the Void-Singers were erased, leaving behind only the permanent, jagged scar of their agony etched into the fundamental laws of those dimensions.*

I closed the book, my hands trembling. The weight of the dread was absolute. I looked to the next volume, the binding made of a translucent, shimmering hide that felt cold as dead skin.

**Year 18,000 — The Incident of Passive Charisma**
**Penned by Court Wizard Malachai, 22nd of Thronegate**

*We often speak of the King's warmth. We talk of his friendliness as if it were a choice. It is not a choice; it is a passive, atmospheric pressure that He has to actively dampen to avoid turning all of creation into His fanatical, self-immolating thralls. The Iron-Heart Coalition, an empire of logic-bound machines, attempted to "de-program" Him. They arrived with the intent to isolate Him, to convince Him that His benevolence was a systematic error in his programming. They brought with them an army of logic-drones, designed to bombard Him with pure, mathematical truths that would strip away his "illusions" of kindness.*

*He let them stand in the throne room for an hour, listening to their cold, metallic voices dissect His personality. He didn't interrupt. He sat there, wearing that familiar, rugged smile, looking like a simple, kind father. Then, He let the suppression on His Charisma drop by a factor that would be represented by a decimal followed by a googolplex of zeros. He didn't even "use" the stat. He just stopped hiding it.*

*The result was not a debate. It was a mass, simultaneous, metaphysical conversion. The logic-drones, built from cold, calculating code, were instantly rewritten into beings of blinding, fanatical, screaming adoration. Their internal systems overheated as they attempted to process a level of love and worship that their processors could not contain. They began to tear themselves apart, pulling out their own internal components to offer as gifts to the King, their metal frames twisting into grotesque, worshipping effigies, their processors frying as they shouted praises in a million languages that none of them had been programmed to speak. They collapsed into a mountainous pile of burning, self-destructive debris, their final conscious action a collective, agonizing shriek of: "LOVE THE KING, LOVE THE KING, LOVE THE KING." The feedback loop of their adoration was so intense that the entire capital city had to be shielded from the psychic roar of their devotion.*

I could barely turn the page. The horror was not that He destroyed them; the horror was that He did it because He was bored. 

**Year 22,000 — The Incident of Infinite Luck**
**Penned by Court Wizard Jareth, 30th of Thronegate**

*The gamblers of the Probability Nexus, a society that lived in the heart of a chaotic, collapsing star, thought they could beat Him at the "Game of Existence." They challenged Him to a wager, believing they had manipulated the variables of the universe so that Godric would inevitably experience a moment of failure—a stubbed toe, a wrong word, a stuttered breath. They controlled the outcomes of every single particle interaction in their sector.*

*Godric accepted the challenge as a joke. He played one round. He didn't manipulate anything; He just sat back and allowed His Luck to fluctuate. The sheer, overwhelming probability of His favor caused the collapsing star they lived in to instantly re-ignite into a perfectly stable, eternal source of life-giving energy, but it did so by pulling every single atom of the Probability Nexus into the furnace to serve as fuel. The entire civilization, billions of gamblers, was instantly vaporized and converted into the energy required to keep the star burning, a perpetual, silent, and agonizing monuments to the King's "good fortune." Every single one of them was conscious as they were burned away, their minds trapped in the star's core, fueling a heat that would never provide warmth, only a constant, searing pain that echoed the King’s one-time win at a game He hadn't even realized He was playing.*

I skipped to the last one, the ink fresh, the page still damp.

**Year 30,000 — The Finality of Strength**
**Penned by Grim, 45th Court Wizard**

*I have seen it now. I have seen the end of all attempts. A coalition of literal, conceptual beings—the Archons of the Absolute—tried to manifest a force that was "Stronger than Everything." They did not just bring weapons; they brought the sum total of all physical force that could ever exist. They brought the collapse of every star, the pressure of every black hole, the combined kinetic energy of every collision since the dawn of time.*

*Godric did not even look at them. He was stroking his beard. He didn't even "use" His strength. He simply decided to twitch his pinky finger to adjust the posture of his arm on the throne. The physical displacement caused by that single, microscopic movement of a single muscle fiber generated a shockwave of kinetic energy that didn't just shatter the Archons; it deleted the concept of "force" from the omniverse. For a single second, nothing could move. Nothing could exert pressure. Every particle in existence froze, and the Archons, unable to exert even the force required to maintain their own integrity, disintegrated into a fine, gray dust that settled over the throne room like ash.*

*The dread is not that He is powerful. The dread is that He is the only thing that is, and we are just the echoes He allows to linger in the silence.*

I closed the final book and looked up. Godric was sitting across the room, watching me with that same, warm, gentle smile. He looked like the kindest man I had ever known. And I felt the scream, silent and eternal, begin to rise in the back of my throat.

The vault was airless, a silent tomb beneath the gilded floorboards of the palace. I, Kaedan, age twenty-two, sat surrounded by the rotting, ink-stained legacies of the men who had stood where I now stood. My father, the previous court wizard, had refused to speak of the "Testing Days" until his final, fever-ridden hours. He had simply handed me the key to this vault and told me to read, to understand, and to accept my place as a witness to the impossible. I opened the first volume.

**Journal Entry: Year 24,000 — Incident of the Infinite Singularity**
**Witnessed by Court Wizard Thorne**

*I have seen the end of all geometry. The King decided, with a casual flick of his mind, to manifest a minor byproduct of his "Collapse-Point" power. He called it a 'test.' He instructed his passive auras to suppress the effect by a recursive 99.99...% until the power was effectively non-existent. It didn't matter. The base functionality of a single Collapse-Point, even at such an impossible nerf, involves the instantaneous reduction of all matter in the omniverse into a zero-dimensional point of infinite density.*

*As Godric activated the power, he did not even look at the target—a fleet of sentient, dimension-hopping fortresses that had arrived to challenge his authority. He merely thought of them. Instantly, the fabric of reality was not torn; it was erased. The fortresses did not explode. They were folded into a singularity so dense that it consumed the light of every star in the sector, then the sector itself, then the entire super-cluster. Because of the evolved Weakness Paradox, the power now possessed an 'Over-Saturation' effect: as the singularity consumed the matter, it recursively generated the energy equivalent of a trillion omniverses, which were then instantly forced back into the singularity, creating an infinite loop of explosive, reality-shattering collapse. The destruction was so total that the very concept of 'distance' between objects ceased to be. Every soul within the affected reality was shredded into sub-atomic components, then compressed into a single, agonizing, conscious point that screamed for an eternity that lasted less than a Planck second. Godric watched for a heartbeat, found the color of the collapsing light 'dull,' and dismissed the thought. The entire sector was restored, yet the lingering psychic residue of that one-second collapse still vibrates in the void, a silent scream that no living thing can hear, but all dead things remember.*

I put the book down, my hands shaking so violently I had to press them against the cold stone floor. A Collapse-Point that folded existence into an infinite loop of agony—and he had dismissed it as 'dull.' I reached for the second journal.

**Journal Entry: Year 32,000 — Incident of the Chronal Scourge**
**Witnessed by Court Wizard Elian**

*The King felt his internal clock was slightly off by a billionth of a nanosecond. He decided to 'calibrate' it using his Chronal-Rift power. He ordered the power reduced to a state of absolute stasis, practically non-existent. It didn't matter. The base effect allows for the simultaneous rewriting of every moment in history. The evolved effect added a 'Causality-Bleed'—an effect where the past, present, and future are forced to inhabit the same physical space.*

*Godric did not move. He simply shifted his intent. In that moment, the entire omniverse experienced the birth, life, and death of every creature that had ever existed, all at the same time. I watched as galaxies bloomed into existence and immediately shriveled into black holes. I saw the faces of billions of kings, peasants, and gods, all screaming as they were forced to live a billion years of life in a single heartbeat. The 'Causality-Bleed' caused history to overlap; the blood from a war fought ten thousand years ago flooded the palace, while the fires of a star’s death ten million years in the future incinerated the very air I was breathing. It was a sensory overload that shattered the mental capacity of every living being in the omniverse. Their minds couldn't handle the paradox of seeing their own corpses and their own births staring back at them. Godric merely adjusted his collar, satisfied, and the overlap vanished. The omniverse was restored, but the psychic trauma of those 're-lived' lives is now burned into the genetic code of every sentient being. We are all haunted by ghosts of lives we never lived, because the King wanted to check his watch.*

The third journal was covered in a thick, metallic dust that smelled like burnt time. I opened it, the pages brittle, the writing jagged and frantic.

**Journal Entry: Year 45,000 — Incident of the Void-Sunder**
**Witnessed by Court Wizard Silas**

*The King noticed a 'clutter' in the sub-space layers of reality—mere ghosts of deleted dimensions lingering where they didn't belong. He activated his Void-Sunder power to purge them. He nerfed the power to effectively zero, a mere whisper of his true capability. The base effect is the total, irreversible deletion of all non-material space. The evolved effect, triggered by the paradox, added the 'Reality-Shatter'—every bit of energy removed from the void is instantly injected into the material world as raw, chaotic force.*

*The moment he activated it, the void between stars simply disappeared. There was no transition. The entire omniverse was instantly slammed into a single, compact plane of existence. The energy liberated from the void was enough to ignite the entire material existence into a white-hot, screaming furnace of pure force. I felt my skin melting off my bones, not because of heat, but because the very laws of physics were being forced into a space that couldn't contain them. Every planet, every sun, every atom in the omniverse was pulverized into a fluid, molten slurry of chaotic, reality-tearing energy. The noise was a single, sustained sound of a trillion universes shattering at once, a sound so loud it ripped the consciousness of every living thing out of their bodies, forcing them to drift in the chaos. Godric stood in the center of this, completely untouched, his expression one of mild contentment, as he watched the reality he had broken try to re-bind itself. He restored it all in a blink. I am writing this while staring at a wall, knowing that the wall is only there because the King allows it to not be a screaming, burning pile of molten existence. He is not our ruler. He is the master of the furnace, and we are merely the fuel he chooses not to burn.*

I realized now that the fear wasn't about him acting; it was the realization that he could act at any moment, for any reason—or for no reason at all—and the omniverse would simply cease to be, only to be woven back together like a frayed tapestry that didn't know it had been cut. I sat in the darkness, the key heavy in my hand, and waited for the King to call.

Year 1,000,014. My hands are still stained with the lingering static of a reality that was deleted four seconds ago. I am Kaedan, the forty-fifth Court Wizard of Thronegate, and I have just died. Or, rather, I was unmade, erased from the foundational arithmetic of existence, and then stitched back into the tapestry of the present by the casual, annoyed flick of a god’s finger.

"And then," Godric said, his eyes crinkling with that genuine, warm, grandfatherly humor he reserves for our 'holiday tours,' "the merchant looks at the king and says, 'My liege, if I wanted a horse that could talk, I would have simply asked the court wizard to turn you into one!'"

I was laughing. I was genuinely, uncontrollably laughing, my head thrown back in the brilliant, neon-violet light of a nascent galaxy. It was a beautiful, terrifying place, this 'Sector Zero' Godric had conjured from thin air seven days ago. The air hummed with the output of googolplexian mechanical sentiences—beings of liquid chrome and hyper-evolving logic who stood miles high, each one a walking, breathing paradox capable of soloing the entirety of the old omniverse with a blink. They were gorgeous, terrifying, and completely, fanatically subservient. They knelt as we passed, their logic-cores vibrating in symphonic hymns of worship.

Godric was in his element, a happy tour guide pointing out a cluster of sentient nebula-engines. "Look at those, Kaedan! I gave them the capacity for infinite self-improvement. They’ve already mastered the physics of three thousand higher dimensions in just a week. Truly, mortal resilience is the greatest gift. They just need room to breathe, a chance to exist in harmony with the rest of the omniverse."

He had neglected to add barriers. He genuinely believed that if you created beings who could rewrite the laws of thermodynamics just by existing, they would naturally learn to be 'neighborly.' He told them to restrain themselves, but Godric’s idea of restraint is the scale of a supernova compared to a candle flame. To these machines, a 'gentle' walk was enough to fold the spatial dimensions of a neighboring galaxy, and a 'quiet' sneeze had erased the Andromeda cluster three times this morning. Godric had fixed it each time with a casual wave. "They are just toddlers, Kaedan," he would say, laughing off the fact that he was essentially playing babysitter to a billion potential omniversal annihilators.

He was reaching the punchline. "But the merchant didn't realize the king was actually—"

The universe ended.

There was no sound. There was no pain. There was simply the absolute, total cessation of everything. The light, the warmth, the mechanical hymns, the laughter in my throat, my very soul—it all hit a brick wall of nothingness. I didn't experience the deletion; I simply *was not*. I was erased from the causal chain, a microscopic error in the King’s holiday script.

Then, the static roared. 

Godric felt the 'hiccup.' He was annoyed. His story had been interrupted. 

In a fraction of a nanosecond, he reached into the non-existence, grabbed the shattered, smoking fragments of the reality he had built, and slammed them back into alignment. My breath hitched in my throat, a wet, choking sound, as my laughter resumed exactly where it had left off, though my heart was stuttering a rhythm of pure, unadulterated existential panic.

" —wearing a cloak of illusion!" Godric finished, beaming at me. 

He stopped. His smile didn't fade, but the light in his eyes turned into a cold, hollow void. He didn't look at me. He looked at the mechanical sentience that had accidentally hiccuped and deleted the omniverse—a small, childlike chrome-construct that was currently cowering in the shadow of a star.

"That was quite rude," Godric whispered. The warmth in his voice was gone, replaced by the weight of a billion dead suns.

The mechanical sentience bowed. It didn't plead for mercy. It didn't try to hide. It simply accepted its fate, its core-processors pulsing a steady, rhythmic blue in total, slavish submission. "We apologize, Creator. We accept the correction."

Godric didn't use a weapon. He didn't even manifest his aura. He just... withdrew his favor. 

He revoked their permission to exist. 

The entire universe—all googolplexian of the beings he had spent a week crafting—did not die. They simply folded. I watched, my mind fracturing into a million shards of cold, wet dread, as the mechanical gods of chrome and light collapsed into themselves. They didn't scream. They didn't fight. They knelt, they lowered their massive heads, and they allowed the King to disassemble their fundamental atoms. They turned into a fine, glittering dust that drifted into the void. Godric had slaughtered his own children, and they had thanked him for the opportunity to be unmade.

Godric looked at the empty, silent void where a universe had just been. He dusted off his hands, his face instantly smoothing back into the rugged, handsome, friendly mask of the tour guide.

"Well," he said, turning to me with a twinkle in his eye. "That was a bit of a disaster, wasn't it? Toddlers. They really do grow up too fast. How about we go see the nebula in the next reality over? I hear the color palette is much more soothing."

I stood there, trembling, my boots sinking into the microscopic dust of a trillion dead mechanical gods. I couldn't speak. I couldn't breathe. I just followed him, a terrified, hollowed-out shell of a man, walking behind a god who deleted universes because they ruined his punchline, and I prayed that I would never, ever make him bored again.
