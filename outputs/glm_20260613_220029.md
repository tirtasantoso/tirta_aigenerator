The morning began, as most mornings did, with me staring at the ceiling of my chambers and wondering if today would be the day my heart finally gave out from the accumulated stress of serving as Lord Godric's court wizard. I am Roth. I am forty-three years old. My strength, speed, endurance, intelligence, and magical capacity multiply by a factor of one thousand every day, and have done so for twenty-three years. By any historical standard, I am a god of arcane destruction, capable of unraveling the fabric of localized reality with a lazy afternoon thought.

I am also, currently, lying in bed with a pillow pressed over my face, making a low, keening sound of mounting existential terror because Lord Godric has just sent me a message through the psychic link he maintains with his court wizards—a link that I have never been able to block, ignore, or moderate in any way—and the message was:

*Roth! I have decided to have a fun visit tomorrow. You will accompany me. It will be entertaining.*

The word "entertaining," when used by Lord Godric, had a specific and horrifying connotation. It did not mean "pleasant" or "enjoyable" in any conventional sense. It meant "I am going to do something that amuses me and traumatizes everyone within observational range." The last time Lord Godric had described something as entertaining, an entire multiversal cluster had been compressed into a marble and I had been unable to sleep for six months.

I removed the pillow from my face, stared at the ceiling for an additional four seconds, and then forced myself out of bed. There was no point in delaying. When Lord Godric decided something was going to happen, it happened. Resistance was not merely futile—it was structurally impossible, and also personally inadvisable, because the only thing worse than witnessing Lord Godric's entertainment was witnessing it while also being on his bad side, a location that no entity with functional self-preservation instincts ever wished to occupy.

I dressed in my court wizard attire—the formal robes that marked my position, embroidered with the ancient symbols of Blake's lineage—and made my way to the imperial sanctum. The servants moved around me like ghosts, their steps silent, their heads bowed. Vael, the former Radiant Sovereign of the Luminal Expanse, offered me tea with the trembling precision of a being who had been personally annihilated and restored by the entity I was about to visit. I declined the tea. My stomach was already performing acrobatics that no amount of chamomile could soothe.

The doors of the sanctum dissolved as I approached, and I stepped into the presence of the King of Thronegate.

Godric was standing in the center of the room, examining himself in a full-length mirror that had not been there yesterday. He was wearing his usual attire—simple, dark garments that stretched across his heavily muscled frame, displaying the ruggedly handsome, forty-five-year-old visage that had been his permanent appearance since the Stasis Event. His salt-and-pepper hair was immaculate. His neatly trimmed salt-and-pepper beard framed a jawline that could have been carved from granite. He was, as always, stunningly attractive in a way that made mortal beings question their own significance.

He was also wearing a pair of thin, wire-rimmed spectacles perched on the bridge of his nose.

I stopped walking. I stared. I opened my mouth, closed it, opened it again, and produced a sound that was somewhere between a question and a whimper.

"Do you like them?" Godric asked, turning to face me with the enthusiastic expression of a child showing off a new toy. He adjusted the spectacles with one finger, pushing them up his nose. "They are part of my disguise."

"Your..." I swallowed hard. "Your disguise, my lord?"

"For the visit! I told you, Roth, I have decided to have a fun visit. We are going to infiltrate the resistance base and observe their preparations incognito." He beamed at me, his warm hazel eyes twinkling behind the lenses of the spectacles. "The spectacles are the key component of the disguise. They change my entire appearance, do you not think?"

I stared at him. The spectacles were, objectively, the most pathetic disguise conceivable. They were thin and wire-rimmed and slightly round, and they sat on his nose in a way that drew attention to his face rather than away from it. They did not obscure his salt-and-pepper hair. They did not hide his salt-and-pepper beard. They did not alter his heavily muscled frame, his ruggedly handsome features, or the overwhelming aura of absolute supremacy that he was presumably suppressing through his absolute concealment.

They were just spectacles. On the most recognizable being in the entire omniverse.

"My lord," I said carefully, choosing my words with the precision of a man disarming a bomb. "The resistance you are referring to—is that the Coalition of Unified Defiance? The one that has been preparing for approximately seven thousand years? The one that believes itself hidden from your awareness through the use of dimensionally-folded pocket realities and quantum-shielded enclaves?"

"The very same!" Godric said, his tone delighted. "They have been working so hard, Roth. So very, very hard. They have constructed the most elaborate preparation in the history of opposition. They have gathered champions from decillions of realities. They have forged weapons of incomprehensible power. They have developed strategies that they believe will exploit perceived vulnerabilities in my defenses. And tomorrow, they plan to launch their grand assault."

"And you wish to... visit them. Today. Before the assault."

"Precisely! I am curious about their preparations. I want to see what they have built. I want to observe their confidence, their determination, their earnest belief that they have a chance." His eyes sparkled with genuine, childlike anticipation. "And I want to do it while wearing a disguise, so that they do not realize they are being observed by their target. It will be like going behind the scenes of a theatrical production before opening night."

"My lord, with all due respect—" I paused, trying to find a diplomatic way to express the fact that his disguise was transparently, hilariously, cosmically inadequate. "Your appearance is... rather well-known. The spectacles may not be sufficient to—"

"Nonsense!" Godric waved a dismissive hand. "The spectacles are a masterstroke of disguise technology. No one will recognize me with the spectacles. I look completely different."

I looked at him. He looked exactly like Godric wearing spectacles.

"Completely different," he repeated, as if sensing my skepticism. "Trust me, Roth. I have given this considerable thought."

The statement was so absurd, so fundamentally detached from observable reality, that I felt my sanity strain at its moorings. Lord Godric possessed absolute omniscience. He knew the exact atomic composition of every entity in the omniverse. He could perceive every thought, every intention, every hidden motive across all of existence simultaneously. And he had concluded that a pair of wire-rimmed spectacles constituted a masterstroke of disguise technology.

I decided not to argue. Arguing with Lord Godric about the adequacy of his disguise would be like arguing with a supernova about the appropriateness of its luminosity. The activity was pointless, the outcome was predetermined, and the process would be painful for everyone involved except the supernova.

"Of course, my lord," I said. "The spectacles are... transformative."

Godric beamed. "I knew you would understand. Now, let us depart. I am eager to see what they have prepared."

The resistance base.

*The gameplay is not about fighting. It is about witnessing. The story is not about conflict. It is about the absence of conflict. The experience is not about overcoming obstacles. It is about existing in a state so far beyond obstacles that the concept itself becomes absurd.*

*I started this game as a skeptic. I expected to find bandwagon hype, overblown praise, and a fundamentally conventional power fantasy wearing a fancy coat. Instead, I found the most committed, most philosophically rigorous, most genuinely transcendent RPG I have ever played.*
