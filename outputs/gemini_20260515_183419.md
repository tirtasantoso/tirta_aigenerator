The *Presence* poured the totality of his creation into a strike of absolute erasure. *Featherine* rewrote the script of reality to ensure Godric was born into a void, not a kingdom. It was a symphony of omnipotent malice.

Godric didn't look at them. He didn't acknowledge the death-scripts. He simply decided to shift his gaze from his tea cup toward the center of the throne room.

The collateral damage was instantaneous. The sheer, kinetic weight of his attention—not an attack, merely a shift in focus—shattered the foundational laws of the multiverse. The entities, who had existed as the highest order of their respective realities, found their ontological stability instantly revoked. Their physical forms, woven from cosmic energy and narrative authority, began to liquefy. It was not a magical death; it was a biological failure of their own existence.

The *Scarlet King* shrieked as his conceptual spines were turned into fragile, screaming nerves that were then rubbed raw by the friction of Godric's gaze. The *Presence* collapsed into a pile of weeping, translucent slurry, his omnipotence becoming a burden that crushed his own spirit into a singularity of pure, visceral agony. *Featherine*’s script-book didn't just burn; it turned into living, biting insects that began to consume her face while she was forced to narrate her own grotesque, eternal dismantling.

"Your persistence," Godric said, his voice barely rising above a whisper, "is a logistical inconvenience."

He didn't strike them. He just allowed his presence to exist in the same space as theirs. The disparity in their status—his, an entity whose power compounds recursively into infinities that make the concept of 'infinity' look like a rounding error; theirs, a bunch of literary characters caught in a web of static logic—did the work. 

They weren't fighting a god; they were fighting the fact that they were *wrong*. 

Godric’s Evolved Weakness Paradox activated. It didn't need to 'win.' It simply absorbed their desire to kill him and converted it into an infinite tax. The entities began to cannibalize one another. The Marvel gods and the DC pantheons were forced by the passive gravitational pull of Godric’s aura to merge into a single, screaming mountain of biomass. They were sentient. They were fully aware. And they were forced to experience their own slow, methodic digestion. 

"Please," the *One Above All* gurgled, his face twisting into a dozen different expressions of terror as he was fused into the chest cavity of the *Scarlet King*. "We... we didn't... how..."

Godric didn't offer a monologue. He didn't offer a lesson. He simply twitched a single microscopic muscle fiber in his right pinky finger.

The movement was so small, so utterly trivial, that the resulting shockwave didn't just kill them. It erased the entire omniverse that housed these fictions. Decillions of multiverses simply vanished. The vacuum left behind was not empty; it was a weeping, bruised reality where the entities were suspended in a state of eternal, conscious nerve-death, stretched across the emptiness like stringy, bloody refuse. They would never die. They would never be erased. They would simply hang there, in the silence of Godric's annoyance, as a permanent monument to the impossibility of their challenge.

Godric looked back at his tea. "The timing was off," he mused to the empty air. "A story needs a proper conclusion, don't you think, Rutger?"

I didn't answer. I couldn't. I was looking at the void where the entire history of fiction had just been slaughtered, and I realized that my King was already bored again. He had cleaned the room, and he hadn't even had to get up from his seat. The arrogance of the multiverse had been silenced, not by a war, but by the sheer, crushing, unfair reality of his existence. I am Rutger, the last wizard, and I know that I am next if I fail to be entertaining. I bowed my head, my forehead touching the cooling, blood-flecked floor, and I began to tell a story. I prayed it was funny enough to keep the omniverse in existence for one more hour.
