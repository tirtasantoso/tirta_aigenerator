Godric had restored everything. The omniverse was put back together perfectly, looking exactly as it had before the battle. The stars aligned, the planets spun, and the people lived their lives. But the original omniverse—the original space-time continuum, the original timelines, and the original, physical matter of everything that had existed before the fight—was *still* compressed, still de-atomized, and still screaming in a state of eternal, conscious agony inside that tiny glass marble that sat on the mantelpiece in the King's private chambers.

Clive looked down at his own hands, his skin, his muscles, and his veins. A cold, suffocating wave of cognitive dissonance and raw existential dread washed over him, making his stomach violently heave. 

If the original reality was still inside the marble... then what was this reality? What was he? 

He was not Clive. He was a duplicate. He was a perfect, identical, and flawless photocopy of a man whose original self was currently trapped, screaming and compressed, inside a toy in his uncle's pocket. The entire omniverse they lived in, the stars they looked at, and the very air they breathed was merely a secondary, simulated copy of existence, created effortlessly by a god who didn't want his home to look untidy. They were living in a shadow, a perfect simulation of life, while their true selves were eternally burning in the microscopic singularity of Godric’s trophy.

The dread was absolute, a heavy, freezing anchor that Clive knew would drag his sanity into the abyss for the rest of his days. He sat in the darkness of the archives, surrounded by the silence of a duplicated world, utterly small, hopelessly useless, and completely terrified of the warm, smiling uncle who was currently carrying their original universe in his pocket.
