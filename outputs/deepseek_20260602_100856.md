And that Godric had been created by a deliberately held-back Stasis Event that the far-future Godric considered "minimal."

And the far-future Godric is holding back again. He is choosing, for the second time in the loop, to grant his past self less than he could grant. And the past self will grow, and amplify, and eventually become this far-future Godric, and then he will hold back again, and the loop will close, and the cycle will continue, forever.

The causal recursion of it is a blade twisting in my skull. Every time the loop closes, Godric chooses to hold back. Every time the loop closes, the past self experiences the held-back version. And because the loop is stable—because the past self always receives the same held-back version—the future Godric who emerges from that past self is always the same Godric who chooses to hold back. The loop is not a spiral. It is a circle. A perfect, eternal, unchanging circle of deliberate, conscious restraint.

"I could break the loop," Godric says, his voice barely a whisper. "I could grant the full version. I could initiate the infinite spiral. The version of me who receives my full current power would amplify upon that base, becoming incomprehensibly more powerful than I am, and then that future version would grant an even more potent version to his past self, and the spiral would escalate beyond any possible conception."

He turns to me, and his eyes are ancient and warm and terrifyingly gentle. "But I do not need to. I am already everything I wish to be. The spiral would be... excessive. Gluttonous. I prefer the circle. The stable loop. The quiet, steady, eternal contentment of knowing exactly what I am and what I will become."

I cannot speak. My voice has abandoned me. My mind is a howling abyss of existential dread.

Godric smiles, and his fingers complete their gesture. In the aperture, the air around the past Godric shimmers, and I know—with an absolute, bone-deep certainty that transcends knowledge and becomes something more primal—that the Stasis Event has been created. The amplification has begun. The superpower genesis has ignited. The absolute concealment has manifested. The Weakness Paradox has activated. The immune system has initiated. The passive shields have formed. The sentient auras, in their nascent, primitive forms, have stirred to life.

And it was effortless. Godric did not strain. He did not concentrate. He did not even particularly focus. He merely extended his will, and the Stasis Event occurred, because his will is the fundamental law of all existence, and existence obeys.

"It is done," he says quietly. He lowers his hand, and the aperture fades, and the Chamber of Infinite Stillness is once again just a warm, pleasant room with comfortable chairs and a trembling plasma-deity.

Godric turns to me, and his expression is the warm, friendly, paternal expression I have known my entire life. "Thank you for being here, Clive. It is... meaningful to share these moments with someone I care about. I remember, when I was the past Godric, how isolated I felt in those early years. The grief, the rage, the sense of being utterly alone in my power. I am glad that I—that he—will eventually find his way to Blake, and to your bloodline. You have been a great comfort to me, across every iteration of the loop."

I try to speak. I fail. I try again. "Your Majesty... I..."

"I know," Godric says gently. He places a warm, calloused hand on my shoulder, and the weight of it is grounding, human, real. "I know it is a great deal to process. The journals did not prepare you for this. Nothing could prepare you for this. But you do not need to understand it all right now. You only need to know that I am the same Godric I have always been. The same king. The same uncle. The same friend. Nothing has changed. The loop has simply closed."

He squeezes my shoulder, and then he turns toward the door. "Come. I believe the Primal Singularity has finished cultivating a new breed of rose, and I am curious to see it. He has been working on a shade of indigo that tastes like nostalgia, apparently. I find his projects endlessly charming."

I follow him. What else can I do? I am his court wizard. I am his descendant's descendant's descendant, stretching back in an unbroken line to Blake, the mortal merchant who stumbled into a hidden sanctuary and shattered a temporal seal. I am his loyal servant, his trusted companion, his nephew-figure who has known him since childhood. I love him with all my shattered, terrified heart.

And as I walk beside him through the palace corridors, past the seamstress who was once a goddess of war, past the footman who once commanded sentient suns, past the chambermaid who was once the abstract concept of silence, I feel the cold dread in my chest bloom into something vast and eternal and absolutely inescapable. The loop is closed. The Stasis Event has been created. And the being who created it—the far-future Godric, my Godric, the warm and friendly king who asks after my daughter's education—is the same being who will hold back for eternity, choosing the stable circle over the infinite spiral, because he is comfortable. Because he is content. Because he is already everything he wishes to be.

And nothing, nowhere, in any possible state of existence, will ever, ever threaten him. Not because he is threatened and survives. Because the very concept of a threat has been rendered meaningless. The loop guarantees it. The circle ensures it. The eternal, unchanging, deliberate restraint of the apex predator is the most terrifying thing I have ever known.

We reach the gardens, and the Primal Singularity rises from the flowerbeds, his form shimmering with barely contained awe as Godric approaches. The roses do bloom in indigo, and they do taste like nostalgia—a gentle, bittersweet memory of things long lost, gently embraced.

"Exquisite," Godric says, and the Primal Singularity trembles with joy.

I stand in the garden, in the warm, eternal light of Thronegate's artificial sun, and I understand that I will never stop being afraid. And I will never stop loving the being who terrifies me. And that is the true horror. That is the final, perfect, inescapable truth of existence.

The loop is closed. The circle is complete. And the apex predator is satisfied.
