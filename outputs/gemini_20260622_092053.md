You were already a helpless, fragile bug to me back then. And now? Now, the gap between us is a wider infinity than the gap between a grain of dust and your entire creation."

Godric Prime did not stand up to strike Him. He did not channel his infinite mana, nor did he use his newly acquired superpowers. He was simply bored of the play. He decided to end it.

With a casual, nonchalant shrug of his broad shoulders, Godric Prime accidentally, passively unleashed exactly zero percent of his physical strength.

The physical deconstruction of the Creator of the universe was a nightmare of raw, unyielding horror. The towering column of golden light was violently, aggressively forced to adopt a biological shape, purely so Godric’s passive strength could inflict the physical sensation of pain upon Him. The Creator's divine form was instantly, brutally transmuted into a massive, towering mass of raw, exposed nerve endings and slick, pulsing muscle tissue.

The transition was an excruciating, tearing process that ripped His consciousness apart. The newly formed, towering column of raw meat immediately began to scream, a deafening chorus of absolute, unadulterated, biological agony that vibrated through the white void. 

Godric’s passive strength did not unmake Him cleanly. He flayed Him. The sheer physical pressure of the air being displaced by Godric’s shoulder movement tensed the muscles of the Creator’s newly forged form, peeling the skin off like wet parchment, the bones splintering and slowly, methodically piercing His own lungs. The divine golden fluid that served as His blood turned into a boiling, white-hot plasma that melted His own skeleton, his eyes popping like grapes under a boot as his brain pan collapsed with a wet, heavy crunch.

The physical shockwave of his 0% strength did not stop at the Creator. It radiated outward, and the entire sector of the omniverse—encompassing decillions of multiverses—was violently, instantly de-atomized. The physical elements of space-time were torn apart like wet tissue paper. All multiverses, all timelines, all dimensions, and all conceptual layers of reality were aggressively dragged toward the center of the vacuum. The sheer, raw physical force of his tensed muscle compressed everything that existed—every single particle, every history, every future, every memory—into an infinite, screaming mass smaller than a single quark.

I watched, paralyzed by a terror so profound my teeth began to shatter, as the entire physical omniverse was compacted into a tiny, pulsing point of absolute density that hovered in the silent, empty void before him.

And Godric simply reached out, his fingers closing around the microscopic speck. He did not seem to exert any effort lifting it; it weighed absolutely nothing to him. He tossed it lightly in his hand, a soft, amused chuckle escaping his throat, before casually, nonchalantly blinking the void back into its restored state.

But the Creator was not restored. He remained as a boiling, screaming puddle of liquefied, steaming divine soup on the balcony stones, His physical structure continuously melting and reforming in a loop of infinite, excruciating physical agony that would never end because Godric’s strength had permanently locked His biology in a state of active collapse.

I fell to my knees, vomiting from the sheer, raw existential dread of what I had witnessed. I looked at the warm, smiling king who was carefully pouring me another cup of chamomile tea, and I knew that there was no higher power. There was no heaven to save us, no divine plan, no cosmic justice. The Creator of the universe was dead, turned into a puddle of boiling golden grease on a balcony, and the warm, smiling uncle carrying chamomile tea was the only absolute truth left in existence.

Clive’s white-haired hands trembled so violently that the heavy iron-bound page of the ledger tore slightly as he closed it. There was no higher power. There was no heaven to save them, no divine plan, no cosmic justice. The Creator of the universe was dead, turned into a puddle of boiling golden grease on a balcony, and the warm, smiling uncle carrying chamomile tea was the only absolute truth left in existence.

The dread was absolute. The futility was final. The gap between them was not a chasm; it was an infinite, double-exponential nightmare of escalating, monstrous omnipotence that would only ever increase, recursively and in an accelerating manner, forever. And Clive would serve his King, faithfully, loyally, and completely shattered by the absolute silence of his own duplicated soul, until the end of time.
