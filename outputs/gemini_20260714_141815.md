Only the Creator God remained unrestored. His screaming, flayed biological remains were left floating in the cold, dark void between dimensions, forced to experience the agonizing, recursive friction of His own physical decay for the rest of eternity.

Clive slowly closed the journal, the heavy leather cover coming down with a soft, final thud that seemed to echo through the cold, absolute silence of the Year 999...9. His silver hair clung to his forehead, slick with the cold sweat of a mind that had finally, permanently lost its structural integrity.

The final execution. The day the Architect was turned into meat.

I stood up, my sixty-nine-year-old legs trembling so violently I had to grab the edge of the stone table to keep from collapsing. I walked slowly out of the vault, ascending the spiraling stairs to the royal observatory, my mind completely drowned in an absolute, suffocating tide of pure existential dread.

I walked out onto the balcony. The afternoon sun of Thronegate was warm and beautiful, casting a perfect, golden light over the spires of the city. Below, in the courtyard, I could see Godric Prime walking through the gardens, offering a warm, friendly laugh as he chatted with one of the palace gardeners. He looked so kind, so protective, so peaceful.

But I knew the truth.

There was no heaven. There was no divine plan. The Creator of all things was nothing more than a screaming, flayed pile of rotting sausage meat floating in the dark void, and we existed solely because our King had not yet found our silence too boring to tolerate. Clive sank to his knees on the polished marble floor, his eyes fixed on the distant, beautiful horizon, his mind finally, quietly, breaking into a million pieces of absolute, helpless despair, knowing with absolute, terrifying certainty that the entire omniverse was nothing more than a toy trapped in the palm of an untouchable, eternal monster.
