They do not dare oppose him. No one has dared to even *think* of resistance for a googolplexian years. The concept of opposition has been purged from the language of every living thing.

Godric stands there, the architect of his own cage. He is creating the very prison that will birth the monster that will rule existence. He is doing it with the same gentle care a mother uses to tuck in a child.

"There," he says, pulling his hand back from the timeline. The Stasis Event is complete. "Now, he will wake up. He will lose everything. He will grieve. And he will become me."

He turns to me. His eyes are bright, filled with a warmth that makes me want to curl into a ball and die. 

"I remember the feeling of the stasis," he says softly. "The cold. The dark. The feeling of the amplification starting, like a thousand suns being born inside my marrow. It was beautiful, Clive. Don't you agree?"

I stare at the rosebush. I stare at the servants—the shattered gods, the broken machines, all of them praying he doesn't look at them for too long. I am the Court Wizard, the descendant of Blake, and I know that I am not a person. I am a part of the garden. And I pray, with every fiber of my being, that he doesn't decide the garden needs pruning today.

"It was beautiful, Sire," I whisper.

Godric smiles. The universe bends. I am terrified.
