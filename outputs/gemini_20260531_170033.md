In exactly zero seconds, decillions of multiverses, infinite timelines, and trillions of civilizations were perfectly rebuilt, placed exactly where they had been prior to the gathering, their citizens completely unharmed, their physical structures pristine.

Only the fictional coalition was left out of the restoration. They remained trapped inside the marble, their pulverized, screaming remains condensed into a silent, eternal soup of liquefied bone, meat, and dead light.

***

Clive’s hands shook so violently that the heavy iron-bound book nearly slipped from his lap, the Pages of Osvald’s journal whispering together with a dry, rustling sound that seemed to mock the absolute silence of the Grand Archive. 

I sat in my high-backed chair, my breath coming in short, shallow gasps, a cold, greasy sweat slicking my skin. I looked up from the parchment, my gaze drifting across the silent, luxurious solar of my King, settling on the small, mahogany pedestal that stood beside his reading chair. 

Resting upon that pedestal, glowing with a soft, translucent violet light, was the marble.

I stared at it, and a deep, shivering, and completely brain-melting ontological horror took hold of my soul, twisting my gut with a sickening, heavy nausea. I looked at the tiny, swirling galaxies trapped within the glassy sphere—the original, compressed infinite mass of the entire possible existence, containing the original Earth, the original Thronegate, and the original timelines of my ancestors.

And then, I looked around the room. I looked at the polished mahogany walls, the crackling fire in the hearth, the cold tea in my cup, and the vast, glittering capital city stretching out beyond the high arched windows. 

If the original, compressed omniverse was still trapped inside that marble trophy, screaming in a state of eternal, high-density unmaking... what was the reality I was currently standing in? 

Is this restored reality real? Am I real? Or are we all nothing more than an identical, hollow, and completely artificial copy, a flawless illusion created by the whimsical, effortless mind of an absolute, terrifying god who could fold and unfold the fabric of existence like a piece of waste paper? The identical nature of the restoration did not offer comfort; it made the horror infinitely worse. It meant that Godric’s power was so unyielding, so ridiculously absolute, that he could construct an indistinguishable, functioning replica of all possible existence in zero seconds with zero effort, while the original remained clutched in his hand as a simple paperweight.

The realization was a crushing, inescapable prison. There was no victory. There was no timeline where resistance had ever been a variable. Godric was the only true reality, the undisputed, eternal apex predator of existence, and we were nothing more than a plaything he kept in his pocket for his own warm, unbothered amusement. 

I closed my eyes in the quiet of the archive, my fifty-year-old mind drowning in a relentless, suffocating tide of pure, unadulterated existential dread, trembling in the presence of my favorite uncle, and hopelessly terrified of the very air I was allowed to breathe.
