The weakest possible configuration. The configuration that had birthed a being who could annihilate the omniverse infinite times with zero effort. Next, I must record the evolved state of the passive shields.

The old journals described Godric's infinite recursive passive shields—layers of conceptual defense that could pre-nullify threats before the threat existed, before a hostile system identified itself, before an enemy understood what it intended to do. Those shields were impenetrable. They made resistance impossible in principle.

The current passive shields are not merely impenetrable. They are predatory.

The shields detect the intent before the first word is spoken, before the first neuron fires in the ruler's brain. And the shields do not attack the ruler. They do not harm him. The ruler finds himself unable to proceed, not because he has been stopped, but because the universe itself refuses to cooperate with his hostile intent.

The shields can also convert attempted danger into ironic punishment. I have witnessed this. A would-be assassin, a being of considerable power who had somehow—through fluke, through blind cosmic chance—managed to evade Pax's probability-scrubbing and Vax's observation, attempted to strike at a Thronegate official. The shields did not block the attack. They converted the kinetic energy of the strike into a self-applied punishment loop. The assassin experienced the exact force of his own blow, applied to his own body, in an infinite recursive cycle that played out over the course of a single Planck second. He survived. The shields are not lethal by default—Godric prefers that punishment be administered consciously, not automatically. But the assassin's mind did not survive intact. He is now one of the silent, trembling figures who tend the gardens of the Eternal Palace, his consciousness permanently hollowed out by the experience of infinite self-murder compressed into an instant.

I have seen him. I have walked past him in the corridors. He does not speak. He does not meet my eyes. He tends the flowers with hands that shake constantly, and the flowers bloom with impossible beauty under his care, because even a shattered mind can be repurposed for service.

The immune system. I must describe the immune system.

The old journals recorded the Year 25,000 incident, when Godric staged a microscopic crack on a dead skin cell and his immune system responded by incinerating the omniverse and restoring it in zero seconds. That version of the immune system was paranoid, overreacting, aggressively redundant. It pre-reacted to threats before they occurred. It maintained infinite recursive backups, each one a full copy of Godric, each copy possessing its own infinite backups. It cross-referenced upgrades across the infinite fractal and synthesized prime versions that made Godric even more invulnerable.

That version of the immune system is, by comparison to the current version, almost quaint.

The current immune system does not merely pre-react to threats. It has evolved to treat the abstract possibility of harm as a hostile entity that can be hunted, cornered, and systematically dismantled. The concept of "injury" is now a fugitive in the omniverse, a theoretical variable that the immune system pursues across all dimensions, all timelines, all states of being, erasing every instance it finds.

If a weapon exists somewhere in the omniverse—anywhere, in any reality, in any timeline—that could theoretically cause a microscopic scratch on Godric's skin, the immune system knows about it. Not because it perceives the weapon, but because it perceives the possibility of the weapon. It tracks probability vectors. It hunts hypotheticals. It corners the abstract concept of harm and devours it, digesting the theoretical framework into immunological data that is then used to make Godric even more invulnerable.

The infinite backups still exist. But they have evolved beyond simple biological redundancy. Each backup is now a full, conscious iteration of Godric, existing in a parallel dimensional layer, and each backup is aware of all other backups. They communicate. They collaborate. They form a hive-mind of absolute immunological surveillance that spans every possible state of Godric's existence.

When a threat is detected—not an actual threat, but the mere possibility of a threat, the faintest shadow of a theoretical framework that might, under impossible circumstances, lead to an inconvenience—the backup hive-mind does not simply overwrite the affected copies. It hunts the possibility itself. It traces the theoretical framework back to its origin point, to the mind that conceived it, to the unconscious impulse that generated the mind, to the cosmic circumstance that shaped the impulse. And it erases the chain of causality.

The being who conceived the threat is not killed. The possibility is simply removed from them. They find that they cannot remember what they were planning. They find that they cannot reconstruct the logic that led them to consider opposition. The weapons they were building melt into harmless composite materials. The strategies they were drafting fade from their documents. They are left confused, peaceful, and permanently incapable of accessing the cognitive pathway that led them toward hostility.

Godric insists that free will be preserved. He genuinely believes in mortal resilience. But his immune system—which operates independently, which he allows to operate independently because paying attention to it would be tedious—has become so aggressive in its pursuit of the possibility of harm that it has effectively erased the concept of meaningful opposition from existence. Creatures can still oppose Godric in theory. In practice, the cognitive pathways required to formulate that opposition have been methodically pruned from the omniversal psyche.

The Weakness Paradox. The evolved Weakness Paradox.

The old journals described a mechanism that triggered when any being, anywhere, even briefly thought Godric had a weakness. Before the thought fully formed, the paradox engaged, granting Godric permanent immunity to the imagined exploit, perfect mastery of the attack, and a massive boost to his amplification rate. Around Year 2,000, the paradox evolved further, triggering even when no new immunities could be granted, causing the omniverse itself to overcompensate by enhancing Godric's power beyond infinitely incalculable mathematics.

That version of the Weakness Paradox is now a distant memory.

The current paradox—which I will call the Paradox Undivided, though Godric has never given it a formal name—no longer requires a trigger. It no longer waits for a being to think Godric has a weakness. It is passively active at all times, across all reality, monitoring every thought that has ever been thought or will ever be thought or could ever be thought by any conscious entity in existence.

If any being, anywhere, thinks about anything—literally anything, not just Godric's weaknesses, but the weather, or their breakfast, or the color of the sky—the Paradox Undivided processes that thought as a potential vector for hostility. Not because the thought is hostile, but because the paradox has evolved so far beyond its original function that it now treats all cognition as a possible framework for opposition.

When it processes a thought, it does several things simultaneously. It grants Godric immunity to any conceivable attack that could be derived from the cognitive architecture that produced the thought. It grants Godric perfect mastery of any strategy, weapon, or concept that the thinking being could potentially access. It enhances Godric's amplification rate—already beyond infinite recursion—by a margin that makes the previous rate look static. And it causes the omniverse itself, in a paroxysm of metaphysical subservience, to overcompensate by adding yet more layers of redundant defense to Godric's existence.

The paradox also performs a function that I can only describe as Conceptual Debt Collection. If a being has ever, in any moment of its existence, harbored a thought that could be construed as oppositional—even if the being did not recognize the thought as oppositional, even if the thought was purely theoretical, even if the being was a child speculating about the nature of power—the paradox marks that being. It does not punish them. It simply notes the cognitive debt, and it waits.

If the being ever acts on that thought, even indirectly, even unwittingly, the debt is collected. The paradox retroactively amplifies Godric's power based on the entire cognitive history of the being's oppositional potential. Every moment of idle speculation, every passing curiosity about whether Godric could be challenged, every unconscious resentment nursed in the dark hours of the night—all of it cascades into a single instant of debt collection that makes Godric stronger and leaves the being permanently aware of their own absolute futility.

The beings are not killed. They are not harmed. They are simply made to confront, in a single, devastating moment of clarity, the mathematical certainty that they could never have succeeded, and that their very thoughts made the object of their opposition more powerful.

I am Clive. I am fifty years old. I have served Godric for thirty-one years. Opposition has ceased.

And he could never use any of it. Not really. Not fully. Not even close. Because the omniverse was too fragile, too impossibly, pathetically fragile, to withstand even the faintest whisper of his true capacity.

The unfairness of it was absolute. The dread of it was absolute. The horror of it was absolute.

And the King was happy. Genuinely, profoundly, eternally happy.

I did not know what to do with that knowledge. I still do not know. But I am a court wizard of Blake's lineage, and we serve, and we record, and we endure.

I am Clive. I am fifty years old. I have witnessed the impossible. And I will never, for the rest of my mortal life, forget the moment when I realized that the most terrifying thing about Godric is not his power. It is his pleasure.

He enjoys being what he is. And nothing—nothing in all of existence—can ever take that enjoyment away from him.
