We stood in the frozen moment for a while longer, the burning pavilion suspended around us, the smoke motionless in the air. Finally, Godric sighed—a soft, contented sound—and the scene dissolved. We were back in the throne room, the infinite palace humming with its ambient, self-aware tranquility.

I was trembling. I, Clive, Fiftieth Court Wizard, with my thousandfold daily amplification, my power that could shatter galaxies, my intellect that could process infinite magical taxonomies—I was trembling like a leaf in a hurricane. Not because Godric had threatened me. Not because I was in danger. But because I had just witnessed the closing of the ultimate circle. The King I served had, in this very moment, become the shadow that had once effortlessly crushed His own past self. He was the Ultimate. He had always been the Ultimate. He would always be the Ultimate. And He was kind to me. He was warm to me. He loved me. And that love was the most terrifying thing in all of Existence, because it was the love of a being who could have prevented His own family's slaughter and chose not to, for the sake of a story He considered perfect.

"Are you alright, Clive?" Godric asked, His baritone laced with mild, genuine concern. "You look pale. Would you like some tea?"

I swallowed hard, forcing my voice to steady. "Yes, Uncle Godric. Tea would be... appreciated."

He nodded, and Kael—the trembling, eternally terrified cup-bearer—materialized instantly, a tray in his shaking hands. Godric took the cup, thanked Kael with a warm smile (Kael nearly fainted from the gratitude), and handed it to me.

"We have a delegation arriving tomorrow," Godric said conversationally, as if He had not just irrevocably altered the fundamental structure of causality. "The Loom-Singers of the Vext Nebula. They've been weaving tribute-tapestries in My honor for a million years, and they're finally ready to present them. I thought you might join Me for the reception. Your father wrote a beautiful poem about the Loom-Singers once, didn't he?"

I nodded numbly, sipping my tea. The warmth of the liquid was a grounding anchor, a small, normal sensation in a reality that had just been revealed to be infinitely more terrifying and infinitely more beautiful than I had ever imagined.

I am Clive, Fiftieth Court Wizard. I serve the Ultimate Godric, the King of Existence, the Apex Predator, the man who allowed His own family to die so that He could become what He is. I love Him. I fear Him. And I will serve Him until my mortal body fails, writing my own trembling entries into the *Tome of Lamentations*, because that is my inheritance, my duty, and my honor.

He has no weaknesses. He has never had a weakness. The one limitation He once possessed—the inability to alter the pre-Stasis timeline—was not a weakness at all. It was a choice. The choice of His future self, now fulfilled, to preserve the story of His own becoming. And now that story is complete. The loop is closed. The Ultimate has walked the final step of the circle, and all of Existence is His eternal, unchallenged, infinitely loved, and infinitely terrified kingdom.

I drink my tea. I steady my hand. I write these words. And I know, with absolute certainty, that there will never be an end to the reign of Godric. There will only be more love, more terror, and more journals.
