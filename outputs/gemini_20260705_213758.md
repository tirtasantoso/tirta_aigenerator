God did not manifest as a soft, abstract light or a peaceful, formless concept. He manifested as a colossal, hyper-dense entity of pure, condensed primordial law. He possessed billions of glowing, multi-winged appendages that stretched across decillions of multiverses, His physical form humming with a golden, universe-erasing energy that made the strongest cosmic deities of our reality look like flickering, pathetic candles. Through His absolute, primordial authority, He saw Godric’s recursive, infinite amplification as a monstrous, cancer-like anomaly that had completely hijacked His perfect design. He had come to retract the "gift of life" from the creature He had created.

Lord Godric stood in the center of the royal plaza, his hands resting casually in his pockets, his ruggedly handsome face tilted slightly upward as he looked at the blinding, majestic presence of his Creator. He did not activate his infinite passive shields. He did not call upon his armies. In fact, a warm, genuine smile of pure, mischievous amusement broke across his handsome face. He looked like an adult who had just run into his old, long-forgotten schoolteacher on the street.

"Well, look at you," Godric chuckled softly, his rich baritone voice echoing through the silent, burning void with an easy, casual hospitality. "The Great Architect. The One who spoke the first light. I must admit, I never expected to see you descend from your high throne. It is a genuine honor to finally meet the artist behind my mortal lungs."

God’s voice did not speak through words; it was an absolute, crushing frequency of pure genesis-law that vibrated directly within the atomic structure of the plaza. *“Mortal. You have allowed your stolen power to corrupt the balance of My design. Your amplification is a disease that threatens the boundaries of the source. I spoke you into existence, and now, I shall speak you into absolute nothingness.”*

Godric’s smile widened, his blue eyes sparkling with a sudden, playful mischief. He had been bored for millions of years. He had had no opposition, no challenges, and no playmates who could survive even the shadow of his afterthought. The prospect of facing the supreme Creator of all reality—the Being who had designed the very concept of power—was the most exciting thing that had happened to him in a googolplexian span of ages. He wanted to see exactly what the Creator of the omniverse could do when He unleashed His absolute, unmitigated maximum.

"You want to erase me?" Godric said, his tone laced with a playful, teasing warmth. "Fascinating. Let us see if you possess the strength to do so. To ensure we have a fair, clean demonstration, I am going to make things very easy for you."

With a single, casual decision, Godric Prime commanded *all* of his passive sentient auras—Gent and all of his more brutal, obsessive siblings—to stand down. He manually deactivated every single one of his infinite, recursive passive shields. He suppressed his absolute concealment entirely within the boundaries of the plaza, exposing his bare, unshielded physical body to the blinding, primordial light of the Creator. He did not brace his muscles. He did not channel his magic. He simply stood there, completely unprotected, inviting the absolute, supreme strike of the Source.

The Creator did not hesitate. Recognizing the absolute arrogance of His creature, God unleashed His all-out, supreme creation-annihilating attack. 

It was the Alpha-Omega Genesis Beam—the raw, concentrated force of the Big Bang, multiplied by the conceptual mass-energy of decillions of collapsing multiverses, condensed into a single, screaming needle of absolute primordial unmaking. It was the force that had spoken the first light into the empty void, a beam designed to retract the conceptual blueprint of Godric’s existence directly from the absolute source of reality, turning his flesh, his blood, his soul, and his memory into a permanent, flat state of non-existence that could never be restored.

The beam struck Godric Prime directly in the chest. 

The impact was marked by a blinding, white-hot explosion of such catastrophic, reality-shattering magnitude that the concept of space-time within the plaza was instantly, violently vaporized. The raw, primordial light of the strike was so intense that my astronomically amplified eyes began to weep tears of pure, boiling blood, my wizard’s brain buckling under the sheer, unyielding density of the Creator's wrath. The physical and conceptual energy of the strike radiated outward, the sheer shockwave of the unmaking beginning to melt the surrounding multiverses like wax in a furnace.

The white-hot plasma of the genesis-beam washed over Godric’s bare skin for what felt like a timeless, agonizing eternity. God poured the absolute, exhausting totality of His divine authority into the strike, His billions of multi-winged appendages vibrating with a desperate, frantic ferocity as He attempted to rewrite the existence of the creature He had designed.

The smoke cleared.

Lord Godric was standing in the exact same spot. He had not moved a single millimeter. His hands were still resting casually in his pockets, his rugged, forty-five-year-old face entirely relaxed. He had let the ultimate, all-out creation-annihilating strike of the Supreme Creator of all reality hit his bare, completely unprotected body, and it had done absolutely, pathetically nothing. He had not even registered a faint, microscopic tickle on his skin cells.

His skin cells—which had been recursively, infinitely multiplying their physical durability and density every single Planck second for a googolplexian span of years—were so monumentally, unspeakably invulnerable that the Creator's absolute unmaking authority had simply bounced off his chest like a gentle, lukewarm breeze, turning into harmless, warm golden sparkles that drifted away on the wind.

The Supreme Creator of the omniverse stared at the unmoving, smiling figure of His creature, and in that instant, God experienced a mind-breaking, absolute existential dread so profound that His billions of multi-winged appendages violently, frantically began to shiver. 

The Creator realized the horrifying, unyielding truth: the creature He had spoke into existence had evolved into a predator so gargantuan, so recursively amplified, and so untethered from the laws of His design, that the Creator Himself was nothing more than a fragile, pathetic dust mote in comparison. His ultimate, absolute strike—the force that had birthed the stars—was less than the lint in Godric’s pocket. The concept of His own divine authority was a laughable, mathematical joke.

Godric let out a soft, disappointed sigh, a quiet, rumbly chuckle escaping his lips as he looked up at the trembling, blinding presence of his Creator. 

"You know," Godric said, his rich baritone voice carrying a cold, merciless playfulness that caused the remaining timelines to violently freeze. "I used to think of you as this terrifying, unassailable mystery. When I was a mortal in Olympus, I prayed to you. I thanked you for my daughters, for my wife, and for the simple warmth of my kingdom. I thought your power was the absolute ceiling of all reality."

Godric shook his head slowly, his eyes narrowing into a mask of pure, unforgiving zero. "But you are so incredibly, pathetically weak. The Godric from Year Zero—the one who had just emerged from his stasis, who was still weeping tears of raw grief for his family, and who had only been amplifying for ten thousand years—could have completely, effortlessly annihilated you with a casual, passing sigh. You are nothing but a fragile child playing with matches."

The Creator attempted to flee. Realizing His absolute, hopeless insignificance, God tried to retract His presence back into the deep, dark void outside the boundaries of the omniverse.

He did not use a spell. He did not draw upon his infinite magic. He simply allowed an infinitesimally small, unquantifiable fraction of his physical strength—a percentage so minute that adding an infinite string of zeros after a decimal point still would not accurately convey its smallness—to express itself through his knuckles.

The physical deconstruction of the Creator of the omniverse was a display of raw, catastrophic violence that permanently shattered my understanding of divinity.

Godric completely, nonchalantly destroyed God, His realm, His laws, and everything associated with Him in less than a fraction of a Planck second, with zero percent of his true power and zero percent of his effort.

I do hope the next era brings us a more creative entertainment, Osvald. Have the servants clean the floor, please. The golden blood is rather sticky."

The Creator of the omniverse, the Source of all light, had been pathetically, brutally slaughtered by the very creature He had designed. There was no higher authority, no divine justice, and no salvation waiting for us in the deep void. There was only Godric, sitting in his garden, watering his roses, and smiling warmly at his terrified servants, while the entire existence was nothing more than a toy in his pocket.
