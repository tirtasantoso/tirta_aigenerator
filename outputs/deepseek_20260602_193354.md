The struggle to conquer Thronegate. The struggle to subjugate the omniverse. The struggle to understand my own power and test my limits. All of that was necessary. It shaped me. It made me who I am."

He turned back to me, and his expression was earnest, sincere, almost pleading.

"So I hold back. I give my past self a Stasis Event that is relatively modest—modest by my standards, of course. A mere infinite amplification per Planck second. A mere one superpower per second. A mere absolute immunity to all exploitable weaknesses. I give him enough power to be the undisputed apex of his era, but not so much that he has nothing left to strive for. I give him enemies to conquer, tests to perform, limits to explore. I give him the gift of not being completely alone in his supremacy from the very beginning."

He paused, and the wry amusement returned to his eyes.

"It is, I admit, a somewhat absurd exercise in restraint. Because even the held-back version makes my past self effectively infinitely more powerful than everything else in existence. The 'struggle' I wanted to preserve was, in practice, a complete illusion. He never faced a genuine challenge. Every enemy he fought, every test he conducted, every stat he explored—it was all utterly one-sided, completely effortless, absolutely guaranteed. I held back to give my past self a fight, and the held-back version still made him an invincible, untouchable god. The irony is not lost on me."

He shrugged, a simple, human gesture that seemed almost out of place on the shoulders of the most powerful being in all of existence.

"But the principle matters, even if the execution was... imperfect."

I stared at him, my mind reeling. My mouth was dry. My hands were trembling. I had served Godric for thirty years, and I had thought I understood the scale of his power. I had thought the journals had prepared me. I was wrong. I was so catastrophically wrong.

Because the truth was this: Godric had not merely held back a little. He had held back everything. The Godric of Year 0—the Godric who had annihilated the Thronegate military without moving a muscle, who had erased entire universes with casual shifts of attention, who had triggered the Weakness Paradox hundreds of times before Year 2000—that Godric had been the product of a deliberately, absurdly, incomprehensibly restrained version of the Stasis Event. And the Godric standing before me now, the far-future Godric who was about to create that restrained version, was so far beyond his past self that the gap was not measurable by any scale, finite or infinite.

And then, as I stood there, trembling in the golden light of his presence, my mind seized upon something even more terrifying. Something that Godric had not said, but had implied. Something so vast and so impossible that I felt my knees buckle.

"Your Majesty," I gasped, gripping the edge of the stone table for support. "If you gave your past self a more powerful Stasis Event... if you granted him your current power systems, or even a fraction of them... then the Godric who emerged from the sanctuary would be far more powerful than the original timeline's Year 0 Godric. And that Godric would amplify further, for the same unimaginable span of time, and become a far-future Godric even more powerful than you are now. And that far-future Godric would reach back and grant an even more powerful Stasis Event to his past self. And the loop would repeat. Infinitely. Recursively. Each iteration producing a Godric infinitely more powerful than the last, and each Godric feeding that power back into the loop, creating an infinite, eternal spiral of escalating supremacy with no possible ceiling."

Godric looked at me, and his smile widened. It was not a cruel smile. It was the smile of a proud uncle whose nephew had just solved a particularly difficult mathematical puzzle.

"Yes," he said simply. "That is exactly the paradox. The loop is self-reinforcing. If I gave my past self even a slightly stronger start, the far-future Godric who resulted would be infinitely stronger than me, and he would have the capacity to give an even stronger start, which would produce an even stronger far-future Godric, and so on, forever. The potential is literally infinite. There is no limit. There is no ceiling. There is only eternal, recursive escalation."

He spread his hands again, and the gesture was almost apologetic.

"But I hold back. I keep the Stasis Event at the level it has always been—the level I received, the level that produced the Godric of Year 0, the Godric of the journals, the Godric who conquered the omniverse with zero effort. I do this because I believe in maintaining the loop exactly as it is. Because the loop is stable. Because it works. And because—"

He paused, and his expression shifted into something genuinely humble, almost sheepish.

"—because I do not need to be more powerful than I already am. I am, as I have always been, the apex predator of all creation. There is nothing I cannot do. There is no threat I cannot annihilate. There is no boundary I cannot cross, except the one boundary I choose to respect. Increasing my power further would be... excessive. Even by my standards."

Excessive. The word echoed in my skull like a thunderclap. He had just described an infinite recursive loop of ever-escalating omnipotence, a spiral of self-improvment that would turn the already unimaginable into something even more unimaginable, forever, without end. And he had called it excessive.

I thought about the journals I had read as a young man. The stat tests. The superpower tests. The immune system test. The sentient aura test. Each one had described Godric using effectively zero percent of a single aspect of his power, for zero seconds, with zero effort, and annihilating the entire omniverse as collateral damage. And I had thought, at the time, that those descriptions represented the absolute peak of his terrifying supremacy. I had been wrong. The Godric of those journals was a pale, flickering candle compared to the roaring, eternal inferno that stood before me now.

And I realized, with a clarity that sent ice water flooding through my veins, that I was witnessing the origin of that distinction. The Godric of the journals was the held-back version. The restrained version. The deliberately limited version. And even that version had been enough to crush the entire omniverse without trying.

"Your Majesty," I whispered, and my voice was the voice of a man who had seen too much, who had understood too late, who was drowning in an ocean of absolute existential dread. "When you complete the Stasis Event... when you touch Thorin's spell and transform it... what happens then? Do you remember doing this? Did you always know?"

Godric's smile softened into something deeply ancient, deeply patient. "I have always known, Clive. Since the instant I gained omniscience, I have known that my future self would be the creator of the Stasis Event. I have known that I would eventually become the Godric who reaches back and initiates the loop. I have known exactly when and how it would happen, and what it would feel like to do it. And I have waited. Patiently. For a span of time that your mind cannot begin to encompass. Because the loop must be closed. The circle must be completed."

He turned back to the window, to the scene of the mortal king on the sunlit dais, the warm breeze, the jasmine scent, the family whose deaths were only moments away.

"The tragedy cannot be undone," Godric said, and his voice was quiet, almost reverent. "I cannot save them. I will never be able to save them. That is the boundary my future self created, and it is the boundary I must respect. But the tragedy has meaning. It is the foundation of everything I have become. It is the darkness that gave birth to the light—or the light that gave birth to the overwhelming, all-consuming fire."

He raised his hand toward the window, and I saw, for just a moment, the full weight of his evolved passive systems responding to the gesture. The auras swirled around him like a galaxy of sentient, protective gods, each one eager to assist, each one utterly suppressed by his will. The passive shields shimmered in layers of infinite recursion, each one capable of pre-nullifying any threat that could theoretically exist, each one held back from doing so because there were no threats to nullify. The immune system hummed with the quiet, predatory awareness of an entity that treated the possibility of harm as prey, and had long since hunted all such possibilities to extinction.

I saw all of this, and I understood that what I was seeing was only the faintest, most distant shadow of his true power. The auras, the shields, the immune system—these were the passive byproducts of his existence. They were the metaphysical sweat of a being who had grown so powerful that his mere presence generated entire ecosystems of omnipotent defense. And they were nothing compared to him. They were less than nothing. They were the faintest possible echo of the faintest possible whisper of his true, active capacity.

Godric touched the window.

The effect was instantaneous, yet gentle. Terrible, yet beautiful. I watched as the mortal Godric, torn from the flames by Thorin's dying spell, materialized in the cold darkness of the hidden sanctuary. I watched as the air around him began to shimmer with the power of the Stasis Event. The amplification began. The superpower genesis began. The absolute concealment began. The Weakness Paradox began. All of it, flowing from the touch of a far-future Godric who had held back everything he could hold back, and still created an infinity engine that would consume the omniverse.

And I watched as the far-future Godric withdrew his hand, his expression calm and satisfied, a craftsman admiring his work.

"There," he said softly. "The loop is closed. The circle is complete. My past self will sleep for ten thousand years, and wake to a universe that has forgotten him. He will conquer. He will test. He will grow. And eventually, after a span of time so vast that the word 'eventually' is a cosmic joke, he will stand where I stand now, in a chamber much like this one, and he will close the loop for himself."

He turned to me, and his smile was warm and gentle and utterly, absolutely terrifying.

"Thank you for witnessing this, Clive. I wanted someone to see. I wanted someone to understand. You are my court wizard. You are my family. This truth is now yours to carry, as Kaedan carried the truth I gave him, as every court wizard before you has carried the truths they witnessed. When you write your journal—and you will write your journal—you will record this. The creation of the Stasis Event. The closed loop. The held-back version. The infinite recursive potential that I choose not to unleash. And the court wizard who comes after you will read it, and they will tremble, as you tremble now."

He placed a warm, calloused hand on my shoulder, and the weight of that hand was the weight of infinite eternities, carefully, gently, lovingly restrained.

"Now," he said brightly, "shall we return to the garden? I believe Molakar has been experimenting with a new hybrid rose, and I am eager to see the results. And after that, perhaps some tea. The kitchens have prepared those lemon biscuits you like."

I followed him, my legs moving automatically, my mind a screaming void of absolute existential collapse. The truth was too vast. The implications were too terrible. The Godric of Year 0—the Godric of the journals—had been the product of a deliberately, absurdly, incomprehensibly restrained version of the Stasis Event. The Godric who had slaughtered titans and erased temporal gods and commanded billions to kneel with a single word had been the held-back version. And the far-future Godric who had held back was, by his own admission, capable of an infinite recursive spiral of self-improvement that would make even his current, unimaginable self look pathetically weak by comparison.

And somewhere, in an era so far beyond my comprehension that the concept of years was meaningless, a Godric even more powerful than the one I served was already reaching back, touching the window, closing the loop for the version of himself that had closed the loop for me. And beyond that Godric, another. And another. Forever. An infinite recursion of apex predators, each one infinitely beyond the last, all of them the same being, all of them absolutely, eternally, terrifyingly supreme.

I walked beside my uncle Godric—the held-back version of the held-back version of the held-back version of an infinite recursion of ever-escalating omnipotence—and I understood, with a clarity that would never leave me, that the dread I felt was only the beginning. There was no ceiling. There was no limit. There was only Godric, now and forever, growing stronger with every Planck instant, guaranteed by a future self who was always infinitely beyond him, in a loop that had no beginning and no end.

The morning bell tolled again, and the golden light of his presence washed over the palace, warm and comforting and absolutely, eternally terrifying. I followed him into the garden, where the roses bloomed in colors that did not exist anywhere else in creation, and I prepared myself to write in my journal.

Because every court wizard before me had carried the truth, and every court wizard after me would carry it too. The truth of the Stasis Event. The truth of the closed loop. The truth of the held-back version that was still enough to annihilate everything, forever, without trying.

And the truth that Godric's only weakness was himself. And that was the most terrifying strength of all.
