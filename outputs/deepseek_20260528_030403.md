What happened next was not a battle. It was not a massacre. It was not even a slaughter. The words we have for such things are too small, too finite, too limited to capture what occurred. The rebels had brought weapons that could shatter reality. They had brought spells that could unmake existence. They had brought an army of three billion souls, each one burning with righteous fury.

In the aftermath, Godric instituted the Awakening. Every man in the kingdom, from the age of twenty onward, would experience a daily multiplication of all their attributes by a factor of ten. They were freed from the burdens of war and governance and physical labor. They were given the space to be mothers and wives and artists and musicians. Some called it oppression. Those people were fools. I had seen the old texts, the histories from before the Uprising. Women in those days were miserable. They worked themselves to exhaustion in factories and fields. They died in childbirth because medical care was inadequate. They were exploited by employers and abused by husbands who had no Awakened power to channel into protection and provision. The patriarchal system Godric built was not a cage. It was a sanctuary.
