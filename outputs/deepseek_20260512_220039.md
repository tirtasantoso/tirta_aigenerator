**Year 700,000 — The Incident of the Superpower: “Conceptual Petrification”**
**Penned by Lord-Mage Solen, 235th Court Wizard of Thronegate**

*I am writing this account with a hand that is perfectly steady, for the simple reason that my nervous system has exceeded its capacity for terror and has simply shut down. I feel nothing. Not the cold of the vault, not the ache of my bones, not the thudding of my heart. I am a hollow shell, a void shaped like a man, and the only thing that fills that void is the image of what I witnessed. The image of the Formless Host. The image of what the King’s superpower did to them. The image of what it continues to do to them, eternally, in the corner of the throne room.*

*The opposition was the Formless Host, a collective of shape-shifting entities who had transcended the very concept of physical form. They were not made of matter, or energy, or even abstract information. They were composed of pure, undifferentiated potential—the raw, primal stuff of existence before it condenses into any specific state of being. They could become anything, adapt to anything, survive anything, because they were, fundamentally, nothing. They had no fixed properties to attack, no defined boundaries to breach, no stable existence to erase. They had drifted through the omniverse for eight billion years, completely untouchable, observing the rise and fall of civilizations with the detached amusement of beings who could never be threatened because they were, in essence, the void between definitions.*

*When Thronegate’s envoys arrived, the Formless Host did not even acknowledge them. They simply expanded their formlessness outward, absorbing the envoys into their undifferentiated mass. Our diplomats were not killed. They were simply unmade—their physical forms dissolved, their consciousnesses scattered into the sea of pure potential, their individual identities erased so completely that they never had a chance to scream. The Host then turned its formless, infinite attention toward Thronegate. It did not declare war. It simply began to drift toward the capital, a slow, patient tide of undifferentiated possibility that would absorb the entire empire without a single act of violence, because violence requires form, and the Host was beyond form.*

*Godric was sitting on the obsidian throne. He had been there for two hundred and ninety days, a span of time that meant nothing to a being who had existed for seven hundred thousand years. He observed the approaching tide of formlessness with the mild, detached gaze of a man watching a particularly boring cloud drift across the sky. He did not stir. He did not activate His magic. He did not summon His auras or deploy His shields. He completely suppressed every single one of His other abilities, reducing His active engagement to a perfect, absolute zero. He allowed only a single superpower to manifest. Conceptual Petrification. But this was not the Conceptual Petrification He had originally acquired. The Weakness Paradox, that eternal engine of cruel escalation, had layered this superpower with effects so devastatingly unfair that even writing about them makes the foundations of my sanity tremble.*

*Originally, Conceptual Petrification was a superpower that could “freeze” a target in place by turning its fundamental concept into an immobile, immutable state—essentially, turning a being into a living statue of itself. The Formless Host believed they were immune because they had no concept to freeze; they were pure, undifferentiated potential. They were catastrophically, laughably wrong.*

*The first evolved effect of Conceptual Petrification is “Conceptual Forging.” It does not merely freeze a target’s existing concept. It forcibly creates a concept for the target if one does not exist, wrenching the target from its formless state into a defined, physical, agonizingly specific form. When Godric allowed the most infinitesimally, incomprehensibly microscopic fraction of this superpower to manifest, the Formless Host was violently, excruciatingly dragged from their sea of pure potential into screaming, bleeding, physical reality. They were not asked what form they wished to take. They were simply *given* a form—a form that Godric’s superpower selected for them, specifically designed to maximize their capacity for suffering. The formless entities, who had never known pain, were suddenly encased in bodies that were nothing more than dense, calcified masses of raw, exposed nerve endings, their every surface hypersensitive to the slightest stimulus, their every movement a symphony of unendurable agony.*

*But the second evolved effect is a horror that even I, after centuries of service, cannot fully process. “Conceptual Locking.” Once a target’s concept has been forged and petrified, it is locked into a state of permanent, unchanging stasis, but with full sensory and cognitive awareness. They cannot move. They cannot change. They cannot adapt. They cannot die. They are fixed forever in the exact state of maximum suffering that the Conceptual Forging created, their minds completely conscious, completely aware, completely trapped. The Formless Host, who had existed for eight billion years as pure, free-floating potential, was reduced to a single, stationary, impossibly dense monument of calcified nerve-flesh, their countless consciousnesses packed together in a screaming, immobile mass, each one experiencing the absolute maximum possible pain for the rest of eternity.*

*But there is a third evolved effect. The deepest cruelty. “Conceptual Propagation.” The petrification does not stop with the original target. It propagates outward along the target’s conceptual connections—any being, any entity, any idea that shared any conceptual resonance with the target. The Formless Host, being pure potential, had touched everything. Their conceptual resonance was woven into the very fabric of the omniverse. The propagation wave exploded outward, touching countless trillions of beings who had never even heard of the Host, simply because their existence shared some abstract, philosophical resonance with the concept of formlessness. I watched through my scrying matrix as entire civilizations suddenly froze in mid-motion, their bodies petrifying into screaming, immobile statues of calcified flesh, their minds trapped in eternal, conscious stasis. The propagation did not stop. It spread across the omniversal fabric, touching dimensions, realities, planes of existence—anywhere that the abstract concept of “potential” held meaning. The petrification wave threatened to freeze all of creation into a single, eternal, screaming monument of itself.*

They remain in the corner of the throne room, a dense, calcified monument of screaming, immobilized nerve-flesh, their countless consciousnesses locked in eternal, unending agony.
