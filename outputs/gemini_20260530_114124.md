The supposedly indestructible infinite shields that the coalition had taken centuries to weave—shields designed to withstand the collapse of infinite dimensions—were instantly, effortlessly shattered like cheap, fragile glass. The shockwave did not just bypass their defenses; it physically crushed the shields, the fragments of their conceptual invulnerability flying backward and slicing through the ranks of the fictional armies like razor-sharp shrapnel.

The physical deconstruction of the Coalition of Fiction was a scene of unspeakable, grotesque, and agonizing detail. The spiky-haired alien warriors, who had ascended to their ultimate forms, were violently, physically pulverized by the air pressure of his step. Their muscles did not merely tear; they were sheared from their skeletons in thick, screaming strips of rendered meat, their golden hair instantly turning to bloody ash as their skulls collapsed inward, spraying the void with a torrent of liquefied, boiling brain matter and shattered bone fragments. 

The comic sun-gods found their invulnerable flesh peeling away like wet paper, their eyes exploding in high-pressure geysers of boiling vitreous fluid as the kinetic shockwave compressed their chests, snapping their ribs and driving the bone splinters directly through their immortal hearts. The pixelated block-gods were instantly crushed into a flat, bleeding paste of pulverized pixels and rendered fat, while the eldritch horrors were violently, physically inside-out, their tentacles shredded into wet, writhing ribbons of raw nerve endings that thrashed in the vacuum of space, screaming a silent, endless chorus of pure, physical torture.

The tech-fleets were reduced to a screaming, fused slurry of melted metal and boiling, vaporized crews, the steel of their ships folding inward like tinfoil under a hydraulic press, crushing billions of soldiers into compact, bleeding singularities of meat and iron. The entire coalition—trillions of the greatest heroes, gods, and monsters from every fictional universe ever conceived—was violently, systematically dismantled in less than a fraction of a millisecond, their screams of triumph cut short and replaced by a wet, bubbling gurgle of absolute, hopeless agony.

But the physical violence of his step did not stop at the coalition. Even with Godric using zero percent of his strength, the passive, unsuppressed kinetic shockwave of his leg movement caused a massive, uncontrollable collateral collapse of the entire surrounding reality.

The entire singular omniverse—everything that existed, everything that could ever exist, all multiverses, all timelines, all spatial dimensions, and all conceptual planes—was violently, instantly de-atomized. Under the sheer, physical displacement of his strength, the structural integrity of atomic bonds, quantum foam, and gravitational constants failed completely. Everything was forcefully, aggressively squeezed inward, collapsing into a single, screaming point of absolute, infinite mass.

I watched through the passive protective shield Godric had extended over my body as the entire fabric of existence was subjected to a horrific, planetary-scale hydraulic press. Galaxies were crushed into stars, stars were compressed into planets, and planets were violently squeezed into atoms, until the entire, infinite expanse of reality was compacted into a single, dense point of matter smaller than a quark. 

The compression was an agonizing, screaming process that I could feel in my own bones. The raw, physical mass of decillions of multiverses, the screaming souls of trillions of dead gods, and the shattered ruins of all history were forcefully packed into a space that had no physical dimensions. The pressure was so vast, so ridiculously high, that the sub-quark point began to glow with a sickly, hyper-dense violet light, emitting a high-pitched, whining frequency of eternal, compressed suffering that vibrated through the absolute void of non-existence.

Godric stood in the empty, silent void of nothingness. He looked down at the tiny, glowing sub-quark of infinite, compressed mass resting on the empty space before his foot. He did not look tired. He did not show a single bead of sweat. He had exerted absolutely zero effort, as if he had simply taken a leisurely stroll through his garden.

He reached down, his massive, calloused fingers gently pinching the sub-quark point. He lifted it with absolute ease, as though the infinite, compressed mass of the entire possible existence weighed absolutely nothing in his grip. With a casual, playful flick of his fingers, he willed the sub-quark to expand slightly, enlarging it to the size of a glittering, glass-like marble. He cast a small, translucent container barrier around it, a barrier designed to act as a seal to stop the marble's infinite, world-crushing gravitational pull from destroying the empty void of the surrounding non-existence.

He held the marble up to his eye, peering through the translucent glass at the tiny, swirling patterns of compressed, screaming reality trapped within. It was a trophy, a small, glittering toy of absolute, condensed suffering that he could keep for his own amusement.

And then, the most terrifying, deranged mechanism of his existence occurred.

Godric became unquantifiably, infinitely more powerful. It was not because he had absorbed the energy of the fictional coalition; their combined, paper-thin power was not even sufficient to increase his baseline by an unmeasurable fraction of a percent. The increase was caused by the Evolved Weakness Paradox.

The paradox, which had been passively active eons before the coalition even launched their strike, had interpreted their attempt to use *fiction and narrative concepts* to defy the King as a supreme, unforgiving insult to his absolute godhood. The omniverse itself, in a display of groveling, terrified apology for allowing these paper-thin, imagined tropes to match their limits against his real, unyielding supremacy, had violently mutated. To apologize for the insult of the fictional coalition, the paradox had aggressively, recursively multiplied Godric’s baseline power by values that completely shattered the concept of infinite infinities, injecting his passive systems with an even more devastating, unassailable baseline of godhood simply because fiction had dared to challenge his reality.

Godric looked at the glittering marble in his hand, then let out a soft, disappointed sigh. "Well, that was a terribly boring show," he murmured.

Without moving a single muscle, without a whisper of incantation or a flicker of magic, Godric simply willed the void to reverse. In exactly zero seconds, effortlessly and nonchalantly, the entire omniverse was restored. Every multiverse, every planet, every timeline, and every star was instantly, perfectly rebuilt, placed exactly where they had been prior to the battle, their populations completely unaware that they had just been de-atomized and compressed into a sub-quark.

But the Coalition of Fiction remained unrestored. They remained inside the marble.

I watched as Godric casually slipped the glittering, glass-like trophy into the deep pocket of his dark trousers, a warm, friendly smile returning to his rugged face as he turned to look at me. "Come along, Osvald," he said gently, his voice once again the rich, comforting baritone of my favorite uncle. "Let's go back inside. I believe the tea is still warm."

I followed him back into the palace, my body shaking so violently I could barely stand. But the true, mind-shattering horror—the realization that has permanently broken my sanity—did not come from the death of the coalition.

It came from the marble.

The marble still sits in the King's private study, a glittering, glass-like trophy containing the compressed, infinite mass of the original, true omniverse. Every single atom of the original reality, every true soul, and every authentic timeline is still trapped inside that pocket-sized container of absolute, eternal compression, screaming in a silent, endless loop of physical agony.

And if the original, authentic omniverse is still sitting inside that marble in his pocket... then what is the reality I am currently standing on? What is this sky? What are these stars? What am I?

We are copies. We are perfect, hollow duplicates generated by his memory, living in a secondary, artificial duplicate of existence while our true, original selves are forever crushed and screaming in a glass toy. Godric has the power to perfectly "restore" everything, but the original remains a trophy. There is no reality, no truth, and no hope. We are just the shadow of a shadow, living entirely at the whim of a god who keeps the true universe in his pocket like a common marble.

Clive’s breath caught in his throat, his sixty-nine-year-old hands slipping from the parchment as the final words of Osvald’s journal burned into his soul. He was a duplicate. Thronegate was a duplicate. The entire, infinite timeline of their existence was just a perfect, hollow copy-paste, while the original, true creation was still sitting as a crushed, screaming speck of infinite mass in the pocket of his warm, smiling Uncle Godric. 

The silence of the vault was deafening, a heavy, suffocating weight of absolute futility. Clive closed his eyes, his mind finally, quietly slipping into the same cold, unyielding madness that had claimed every single court wizard who had ever dared to look behind the curtain of the King’s gentle, terrifying smile.
