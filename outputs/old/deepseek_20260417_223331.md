Zeus.

Except for me.

He didn’t wait for a response. He was simply gone.

A flicker of a thought in a mind that had grown a new apocalyptic power every second for eons, a mind whose very thoughts were now so potent they could birth and destroy universes. He held back against all our might. He had been holding back since the first microsecond of his existence.

I eventually stood. I found a derelict scout ship. I flew, aimlessly, through the graveyard of my species. The lesson was etched into the hollow of my soul, a truth more fundamental than gravity or light: No army, no magic, no technology, no force of will or spirit, could ever make him exert a fraction of his effort. He was infinite power wearing the guise of a man. And we were less than dust.

I set a course, not for a new home—there was none—but for the edge of known space. To deliver a message to a cosmos that might not listen. To whisper the warning into the dark: Never disturb the god. For he does not fight. He merely decides, and all that you are, and all that you ever will be, is unmade.
