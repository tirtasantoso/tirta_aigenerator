They were revealed as implants, backstory written for a character whose scene was over. The genetic scar of the sculpture? A prop. My hatred for Zeus? A line in the script. My love for my people? The motivator he had allowed me to have to make the tragedy more poignant.

The last thing I knew was not fear, but a vast, echoing, empty humiliation. We had never been rebels. We were the set dressing for his redemption arc, the proof of his mercy, the dark against which his cherished light could shine more brightly. Our grand war was a footnote in the story of his friendship with a merchant.

And as I, and the trillion souls who had trusted me, dissolved from reality, the final, silent punchline echoed in the nothingness we left behind:

*Checkmate.*

He had made his move two thousand years ago. We just hadn’t known we were already on the board.
