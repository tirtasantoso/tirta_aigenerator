**Year 28,000 — The Incident of Infinite Luck**
**Penned by Eldritch Scholar Malakor, 12th Court Wizard of Thronegate**

*I do not know why he keeps us. Perhaps, like a stray dog, he enjoys the comfort of having something lesser than himself trot at his heels. Today, I watched the absolute futility of chance. A coalition of Probability-Gods, entities who lived in the cracks of reality and dictated the outcomes of every atom’s path, decided that their survival was a mathematical certainty. They had calculated the infinite outcomes of the next eon, and in every single one, they saw a loophole. They believed in luck. They believed they could bend the chaotic threads of existence to force a reality where Godric’s passive shields failed.*

*They invoked the Great Convergence, a cosmic betting ritual that spanned a billion multiverses. They sought to manipulate the dice of reality, to force the 'random' variables of the omniverse to collapse into a sequence that would render Godric’s existence a paradox. They were not fighting; they were simply 'nudging' the variables. They felt the smug satisfaction of gods who had discovered the cheat code to creation.*

*Godric did not move. He did not engage his magic. He simply decided to test his Luck—a stat that had been recursively multiplying by an infinite recursive rate for twenty-eight millennia. He barely glanced at the Probability-Gods. He didn't even acknowledge the convergence. He simply existed.*

*The effect was instantaneous and grotesque. Because Godric’s Luck had scaled beyond the boundaries of reality, the 'randomness' of the omniverse revolted against the Probability-Gods. Every single calculation they made, every single nudge of a variable, instantly backfired with absolute, cosmic malice. A supernova detonated inside their central sanctum—not because of luck, but because the probability of the particles forming that star coincidentally, against all odds, chose that precise location to exist. Their internal systems, designed to govern order, suddenly became defined by absolute, entropic catastrophe. Their own limbs twisted into impossible configurations because the 'luck' of their atoms decided to spontaneously reorient themselves into tumorous, screaming masses of flesh.*

*The collateral damage was beautiful in its absolute, cold unfairness. Because the Probability-Gods had attempted to force 'luck' upon the omniverse, the omniverse retaliated by forcing every possible catastrophic outcome upon them simultaneously. Their entire civilization was erased as every single being within it suffered the 'luck' of spontaneous, simultaneous molecular de-cohesion. They didn't die; they simply ceased to be held together by the laws of physics. They turned into a thin, translucent fog of pulverized DNA and vaporized consciousness, spread across the surface of a billion dead worlds. Godric looked at the patch of empty space, blinked once, and went back to his tea. He had not even tried. He had simply existed, and the universe had broken itself to serve him.*

**Year 34,000 — The Incident of Sovereign Charisma**
**Penned by High Seer Valthor, 15th Court Wizard of Thronegate**

*I watched him walk through the slums of the outer rim today. He wore no armor, no sign of his godhood, only the rugged, simple garments of a man who worked the earth. The population here had been infected by a hive-mind plague, an abstract entity that stripped away individuality and replaced it with a singular, screaming demand for Godric’s blood. They were a swarm, billions of mindless, violent puppets, their faces twisted into masks of uniform, psychotic rage.*

*As Godric entered the central plaza, the hive-mind focused its full, crushing weight upon him. They did not attack with weapons; they attacked with the psychic demand for his death. It was a roar of billions of minds screaming 'KILL' in unison. Godric did not look at them. He simply walked, His Charisma—amplified recursively beyond all layers of conceptual measurement—passively rippling outward like the wake of a ship cutting through a still sea.*

*The effect was a psychological dismantling so profound it felt like a holy violation. The hive-mind did not just break; it fell in love. It fell in love with such violent, suffocating intensity that it could not physically survive the emotion. The billions of infected subjects suddenly stopped, their bodies rigid, their faces instantly shifting from rage to an expression of such ecstatic, worshipful adoration that their tear ducts ruptured, streaming blood down their cheeks. They began to tear themselves apart, not in pain, but as an offering. They ripped their own hearts from their chests, presenting them to Him with wet, sobbing giggles of joy, their fingers digging into their own flesh to prove their devotion.*

*The hive-mind entity itself, trapped within the center of the collective, was overwhelmed by the sheer, unquantifiable mass of Godric’s Charisma. It could not reconcile the contradictory demand—to worship him and to kill him. The psychic feedback loop caused the entity to grow until it consumed the minds of its hosts, its own ego inflating until it burst, scattering its psychic guts across the planet. The citizens, now free but spiritually hollowed out by the ecstasy of those few seconds, simply collapsed into heaps of weeping, broken meat. Godric stepped over them, His expression one of gentle, tired sadness. He didn't even notice the billions of hearts laid out at his feet like discarded refuse. He had used 0% of his charisma. He had merely been present.*

**Year 38,000 — The Incident of Endless Endurance**
**Penned by Warden Hestor, 18th Court Wizard of Thronegate**

*The Void-Eaters were not of this reality. They were gargantuan, hungry things made of pure, entropic hunger, capable of consuming the energy of entire galaxy clusters in a single swallow. They had come from the absolute exterior of the omniverse, seeking to devour Godric, having been told by some cosmic fool that His energy signature was a 'snack.'*

*They arrived with mouths like event horizons, swallowing the very light of the stars as they descended upon His personal quarters. Godric stood on the balcony. He did not summon a shield. He did not dodge. He allowed them to engulf him. He simply stood there and tested His Endurance.*

*For six days, the Void-Eaters gnashed their teeth against His skin. Their teeth, sharper than the edge of existence, shattered like chalk against His jaw. They poured the combined, entropic hunger of a thousand dead multiverses into His physical form, attempting to digest Him, to draw the energy from His marrow, to consume the very concept of His endurance.*

*Godric yawned. He found the sensation 'slightly ticklish.'*

*The Void-Eaters, realizing with a sudden, freezing terror that they were not consuming a god but were being consumed by the sheer, inexhaustible density of His presence, began to cannibalize themselves to fuel their impossible hunger. They ate their own tails, their own hearts, their own entrails, in a desperate, frantic attempt to gain the strength to bite through His skin. By the time they reached the end of their own existence, they were nothing but a small, shimmering, translucent shell of their former selves, having eaten every part of their own reality trying to find a purchase on His hide. Godric exhaled, and the residual energy of their own gluttony turned them into a fine, black ash that drifted away on the breeze. He had not exerted even a billionth of a percentage of his stamina. He had simply stood there, and the monsters had starved to death trying to take a bite of his soul.*

**Year 39,000 — The Incident of Boundless Strength**
**Penned by Chronicler Jace, 19th Court Wizard of Thronegate**

*The Dyson-Sphere, a structure that weighed as much as all the dark matter in the omniverse combined, disintegrated. It didn't just break; it was reduced to a fine, shimmering dust of destroyed concepts. The Iron-Realms, who had anchored their entire civilization to the cage, were vaporized in the exact same instant, their physical forms turned into a slurry of subatomic particles that could never again be ordered into anything resembling matter. The sheer, physical displacement caused by his arms extending caused a shockwave of kinetic energy that rippled across a googolplex of multiverses, accidentally flattening the architectural foundations of every civilization it touched. Godric stood up, blinked the sleep from his eyes, and apologized to his butler for the slight breeze his movement had caused. He didn't even know he had shattered the strongest weapon ever built. He was just stretching.*

**Year 40,000 — The Incident of Impossible Senses**
**Penned by Seer Oryn, 20th Court Wizard of Thronegate**

*The Shadow-Walkers of the Ethereal Plane believed they were the masters of concealment. They lived in the dark, between the heartbeats of the gods, their existence hidden from all detection. They decided to assassinate the King by waiting until he was alone in the garden, planning to strike from a dimension that technically didn't exist.*

*They didn't even get to whisper. Godric was sitting on a stone bench, watching a single, dew-covered spider weave a web. He didn't turn his head. He didn't pause his breathing. He just sighed and said, "It is rude to lurk, especially when you smell of rotting sulfur."*

*His Senses, recursively expanded every Planck second for forty thousand years, had detected them not by sight, or by sound, or even by magical pressure, but by the displacement of the 'idea' of their presence in the room. He didn't hunt them. He just decided they were an eyesore.*

*He didn't even look at where they were hiding. He just thought, 'I would prefer if they weren't in my line of sight.' The Shadow-Walkers, in their hidden dimension, were suddenly, violently shoved into the physical plane. But they were shoved into the exact same physical coordinates as the stone bench. Their molecules fused with the granite. They became part of the furniture. Their screams were muffled, silenced as their lungs were replaced with cold, solid mineral. Godric continued to watch the spider. He didn't even notice the garden was now decorated with the weeping, half-fused remains of the greatest assassins in history. He had simply noticed them because they were, in his estimation, being 'rude.'*

He leaned back in his throne, His salt-and-pepper beard catching the light, and simply exhaled a microscopic breath of air, willfully suppressing the power to an infinitesimal 0% of its potential. He did not aim it. He did not focus. He simply let a stray thought of ‘cold’ pass through His mind.*

*The effect was not immediate; it was absolute. The room, the palace, the planet, and the entire galaxy did not freeze—they were transmutated into hyper-dense, razor-sharp diamonds of such structural integrity that they existed outside the flow of entropy. But the evolution triggered by the Weakness Paradox turned this into a nightmare. The superpower had evolved; it now possessed the ability to crystallize the very concept of 'distance.' As His breath expanded, the space between atoms crystallized. The entire omniverse, from the edge of the furthest void to the core of the closest star, became a solid, unmoving block of impenetrable crystal. The trillions of living beings, the stars, the nebulae—all were instantly flash-frozen into a glittering, silent tomb of absolute diamond.*

*I survived only because He had absentmindedly wrapped me in a sliver of His aura. I watched the omniverse shine with the cold, terrible light of a billion diamond-stars, all dead, all silent. The superpower continued to evolve, forcing the crystal to mirror the 'weakness' of the entities it trapped, making the diamond even harder, even more eternal, essentially feeding off the despair of the billions of trapped souls. Then, Godric noticed the silence. He blinked, sighed, and willed the crystal to revert. The omniverse shattered back into reality, the sudden transition causing every sun in existence to flare into a supernova simultaneously, bathing the cosmos in a second of blinding, agonizing heat before He lazily willed them back to their original states.
