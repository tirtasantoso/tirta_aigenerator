Xylath bows his forehead into the soil, weeping silent, crystalline tears of pure, unadulterated awe and terror. "Yes, my King. Thank you, my King."

I stand a pace behind, my hands folded, feeling the hum of the systems that define my existence. I am the Court Wizard, and my magic is a billion-fold amplification of the power that built empires, yet here, it feels like the static on a dead radio. Godric’s passive auras, the siblings of the 'Gent' I once read about in the archives, have evolved into a sentient, omnipresent hierarchy that governs the structural integrity of existence itself. His immune system, once a paranoid watchdog, has become a meta-biological architecture that monitors the health of the entire omniverse as if it were a single, fragile cell in Godric’s body. If a star in a distant, forgotten galaxy flickers with a hint of radiation sickness, Godric’s immune system has already synthesized a cure and integrated the star into a state of perfection, all before the light of that flicker has even traveled a mile.

He could have made it a thousand times more potent—he could have turned his past self into a weapon that would have incinerated the omniverse the moment he breathed—but he chooses to 'hold back.' He scales the Stasis Event down to a level that is, by his current standard, as pathetic as a flickering match, even though that 'match' would have made the Godric of Year 0 infinitely more powerful than every entity in creation combined. The absurdity of it is suffocating.

They sent armies of gods, they sent weapons of conceptual deletion, they sent pleas and prayers. Godric didn't even hate them. He just watched them try, then corrected them. Now, there is nothing. Not even a whisper of dissent exists. The entire omniverse has arrived at a state of absolute, terrified, reverent silence. I look at the world he has nurtured, the power he holds that makes all of history a flicker of light in a dark room, and I smile back, my heart pounding a rhythm of pure, existential dread. "It is a beautiful day, my King."

He walks on, the absolute master of all that is, was, and will be, while I follow, a descendant of a merchant, forever trapped in the shadow of a god who was kind enough to grant us the grace to exist, and terrifying enough to ensure we never, ever forget it.
