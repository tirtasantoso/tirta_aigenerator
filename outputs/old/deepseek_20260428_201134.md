The first thing the new king of Thronegate did upon waking was remember the smell of his daughter’s hair. It was a cruel trick of the mind, for Godric had not possessed a mind that could be tricked in ten thousand years. He stood in the ruins of a sanctuary that had been forgotten before the first stone of Thronegate’s capital had been laid, and he remembered Isabel’s laughter, the way it used to ring across the marble halls of Olympus like a bell forged by joyful gods. He remembered Hebe, too, her serious little face as she tried to master a hunting bow twice her size. He remembered Reina, his wife, the way she would place her hand on his chest before he went to war and say nothing at all, because nothing needed to be said.

The man who had stumbled upon this place was named Blake, and he was covered in dust from the collapsed entrance he had inadvertently triggered. He was thirty-eight years old, with a friendly mutton chop that framed a face more accustomed to haggling over spice prices than confronting ancient ruins.
