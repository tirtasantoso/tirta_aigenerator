Zeus reached out and caught the man’s throat with a casual, fatherly grip. He didn't squeeze. He simply shared a fraction of his own omniscience with the warrior. I watched the Saiyan’s eyes melt as his mind tried to process the infinite scale of Zeus’s power. His brain couldn't handle the data of a billion-fold amplification per second; his skull fractured from the internal pressure of his own expanding thoughts, and Zeus let the lifeless husk drift away into the void.

The battle, if you could call it that, had lasted exactly four seconds. The 'strongest' beings from a thousand popular fictions were now nothing more than a slurry of gore and broken spirits floating in the vacuum. Zeus turned back toward the palace, his expression one of mild disappointment, as if he had been interrupted during a pleasant nap. He looked at us on the battlements and offered a small, friendly nod. "Clean this up, Roger," he said, his voice warm and fatherly, as if he hadn't just erased the champions of a billion worlds. "It’s untidy, and Blake’s descendant is coming over for tea later. I don’t want the boy seeing such a mess." I bowed low, my forehead touching the cold stone, feeling the terrifying, beautiful weight of his gaze. We are the men of Thronegate, titans in our own right, but as we began the gruesome task of incinerating the remains of gods and superheroes, we knew the truth. There is no resistance. There is no hope for the rebellious. There is only Zeus, and the infinite, compounding silence of his absolute reign.
