Godric stood at the altar, his broad shoulders draped in a ceremonial cloak of white and gold that was rapidly becoming saturated with the lifeblood of his kin. He had been looking into the eyes of Isabel, his twenty-two-year-old daughter, as he prepared to join her hand with her betrothed. Behind her, the fifteen-year-old Hebe was a crumpled heap of broken limbs, and Queen Reina, the light of Godric’s soul, had been silenced while reaching for her children.

The kingdom of Thronegate had not declared war; they had simply arrived like a plague. Their soldiers moved through the palace with a cold, mechanical efficiency, slaughtering the wedding guests and the royal guard with equal indifference. Godric roared, a sound of such profound agony and rage that it seemed to vibrate the very foundations of the world, but he was pinned by a dozen enchanted spears, his legendary strength failing against the sheer weight of the betrayal. It was the forbidden teleportation spell of his bloodline, a magic designed to fold space and time to preserve the king at any cost.

The incantation was halfway to completion when a Thronegate spear pierced Thorin’s chest from behind. The wizard coughed a spray of crimson, his focus shattering. The spell, robbed of its anchor and its architect, did not fail entirely; instead, it curdled. Reality around Godric didn't just bend; it tore. Simultaneously, his soul began to manifest apocalyptic superpowers at the rate of one per second. The first second gave him the power of Absolute Concealment, an ability that allowed him to perfectly suppress his own aura and physical presence. This concealment power, like all others he would gain, began to scale at the same terrifying rate as his base stats. He was no longer the cold, merciless engine of destruction he had been upon his awakening. The presence of Blake had changed him. Beside him stood Blake, his loyal right-hand man, leaning on a staff that looked remarkably like the one Thorin had carried.
