I believe the Coalition leaders are currently waking up from their bliss-storage pods, wondering why they feel an inexplicable urge to send me a thank-you card. I should probably give them a little speech. Something reassuring. ‘You tried your best and I appreciate the effort, now please consider surrendering and we can get to the integration phase.’ I’ll have the scribes draft something. But first, let’s finish our breakfast.”

He sat back down and picked up the butter knife.

I, Leon, High Court Wizard, descendant of Blake, sat across from him and said nothing. The existential dread was not a thing I could put into language. The silence inside me was the silence of the void that had been cleaned, and the worst part—the part that I will take to my grave, the part that will follow me into whatever afterlife exists for people like me—was that I still loved him. I still revered him. He was the best king the omniverse could ever ask for: kind, just, fair, genuinely concerned with the welfare of his subjects. And he was also the absolute, undeniable, infinite proof that nothing any of us did would ever matter unless he willed it so. The sacrifice of the omniverse had been real. The pain, the hope, the desperation—those were real. But their meaning was wholly dependent on his whim. He could allow a scratch and then erase its consequences with a snap. The only thing that gave their actions any weight was his permission, and that was not liberty; it was a gilded cage so vast that its bars were invisible.

I will never sleep well again. I will perform my duties, and I will stand beside my king, and I will smile when he tells his terrible jokes about the Ontological Sanitization Cascade, and I will drink the lemon water served by the entity that could annihilate me with a stray thought. And I will remember that fifteen thousand years of resistance led to a staged scratch on a dead skin cell, and that the only reason the omniverse exists right now is because Godric felt like having a croissant and wanted some company.

That is the truth of the Tyrant Godric. That is the horror I carry. And you, whoever reads this chronicle, will never understand the full weight of it, because the part of your mind that could comprehend it has already been sanitized by the truth itself.

The croissant, by the way, was delicious. That is the ultimate dissonance. Even the pastry is perfect.
