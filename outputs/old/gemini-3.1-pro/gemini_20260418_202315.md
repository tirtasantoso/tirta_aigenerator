Beside him floated Doctor Manhattan, a naked, glowing blue giant whose very presence caused the quantum strings of space-time to weep and bend. Flanking them was Son Goku, wrapped in the blinding, silver-white flames of Mastered Ultra Instinct, his energy so suffocatingly dense it caused the surrounding stars to flicker and die. Saitama, a bald human in a yellow jumpsuit, stood upon a fragment of a shattered asteroid, his face completely blank, absentmindedly cracking his knuckles. Hovering safely in the rear was the golden, ethereal apparition of Giorno Giovanna and his reality-bending phantom, Gold Experience Requiem, a force capable of resetting any action to zero.

The combined ambient pressure of these ungodly beings cracked the obsidian battlements beneath Roger’s boots. Superman Prime drifted forward, his voice a booming, righteous thunder that carried across the vacuum of space. “Your endless reign of terror stops today, Zeus,” Clark declared, his eyes glowing with an unbearable, apocalyptic heat. “We have observed your slaughter from beyond the source wall. We represent the indomitable spirit of a trillion realms. Stand down and face the judgment of the multiverse.” 

A gentle, rhythmic clicking echoed from the courtyard behind Roger. The maintenance worker instantly threw himself to his knees, pressing his forehead against the freezing stone. Zeus strolled casually onto the battlements, wearing a simple, tailored black tunic that clung to his muscular, immaculately preserved forty-five-year-old physique. His neat salt-and-pepper beard framed a face completely devoid of concern. He didn't carry a weapon. He didn't even emit a glow. He simply looked at the assembled gods of fiction the way a man might observe a particularly stubborn stain on a carpet. “Judgment,” Zeus murmured, tasting the word on his tongue. He offered a warm, chilling smile. “How wonderfully nostalgic. I have not heard a threat so utterly devoid of comprehension in at least four hundred millennia.”

Goku stepped forward, the silver aura exploding around him in a blinding sphere of pure martial perfection. “I don’t care about ruling or empires,” Goku said, his voice echoing with a slight, battle-hungry vibration. “But I hear you’re the absolute strongest guy who ever lived. I’ve been training in the void for a million years just to test your limits. Don’t hold back!” 

Doctor Manhattan’s glowing white eyes narrowed as he focused his absolute omniscience upon the god-king. The blue entity observed the infinite, compounding layers of Zeus’s biology. The fatal error occurred in absolute silence. Manhattan’s boundless intellect formed a singular, passing thought: he hypothesized that Zeus’s physical form, while compounding infinitely, was still fundamentally anchored by subatomic tachyon fields. He believed that if he dismantled those specific tachyons, the king’s structure would collapse. In that exact same microsecond, Giorno Giovanna’s mind formulated a parallel strategy. Giorno observed Zeus’s breathing and reasoned that every action Zeus took had a cause and an effect, a temporal flaw that Gold Experience Requiem could instantly exploit by reverting the king’s will to zero. Saitama and Goku, operating on pure martial instinct, simultaneously perceived that Zeus had a physical shell, a finite boundary of flesh that could theoretically be breached if hit with enough infinite force. 

The Weakness Paradox violently snapped shut across the fabric of the omniverse. By daring to conceptualize a weakness—by merely forming the subconscious belief that Zeus possessed a subatomic vulnerability, a temporal cause-and-effect boundary, or a physical limit to his durability—these supreme entities unknowingly signed their own horrific death warrants. In the space of a single Planck length, Zeus’s already infinite amplification rate surged significantly higher forever in direct response to their collective insult. Absolute immunity to subatomic manipulation, causality reversal, and infinite blunt-force trauma was permanently inscribed into his being. Simultaneously, Zeus achieved supreme, permanent mastery over quantum unmaking, causality manipulation, and martial force, completely denying the very existence of these concepts to the attackers. 

Saitama was the first to move, instantly crossing the cosmic void with a punch that carried the kinetic weight of a billion expanding big bangs. At the exact same moment, Goku unleashed a transcendent, galaxy-shattering strike directly at Zeus’s skull. Clark Kent plunged from the heavens, his fists burning with the heat of the prime cosmos. Doctor Manhattan waved his blue hand, attempting to instantly unmake Zeus’s atomic structure. Giorno’s golden phantom screamed, unleashing the absolute power of Return to Zero to strip Zeus of his reality. 

Zeus did not even blink. He did not raise his hands. He did not shift his weight.

When Saitama’s fist made contact with the invisibly thin, infinitely compounded veil of Zeus’s passive aura, the absolute disparity in their existences violently rejected the attack. Saitama’s arm did not break; it completely liquefied. The infinite kinetic force of his own punch rebounded inward, traveling up his shoulder and instantly transfiguring his skeleton into a pressurized geyser of pulverized bone. Saitama’s blank expression contorted into sudden, unfathomable agony before his entire body violently exploded into a cloud of red, atomized mist. Goku’s transcendent silver strike met the same fate. The Saiyan’s indestructible cellular structure violently inverted. His bones tore out of his own chest, bursting through his orange gi as the sheer gravitational pressure of Zeus’s existence crushed his organs into dense, microscopic black holes. Goku’s screaming jaw was entirely ripped from his face by his own rebounding momentum before he was completely erased into a shower of boiling blood. 

Clark Kent collided with the aura, and the golden, indestructible sunlight in his veins was instantly transmuted into highly radioactive, jagged glass. The Man of Steel shrieked—a sound that shattered the nearby moons—as his invulnerable flesh boiled away, leaving nothing but a charred, screaming skeleton that crumbled into gray ash before it even hit the stone floor. 

Hovering above the slaughter, Doctor Manhattan froze. The blue god experienced the first and last moment of true, blinding terror in his immortal life. He reached out to unmake Zeus, only to realize with absolute, mind-shattering horror that the very concept of quantum manipulation no longer existed for him. Zeus effortlessly invoked his newly acquired mastery over quantum physics. The blue god did not just die; he was agonizingly peeled apart layer by layer, his omnipresent consciousness violently dragged through a microscopic singularity of infinite pain. Manhattan screamed, his blue light flickering into absolute darkness as he was conceptually wiped from the tapestry of all timelines. 

Giorno Giovanna watched his invincible, golden phantom freeze mid-air. Gold Experience Requiem began to weep thick, black blood from its eyes before it shattered like brittle glass under the sheer weight of Zeus’s causality mastery. Zeus forcefully denied Giorno’s existence, instantly compressing the boy’s infinite death loop into a single, agonizing microsecond. Giorno’s body ruptured from a trillion simultaneous fatal wounds, folding inward into a grotesque, fleshy sphere of bursting eyeballs and shattered teeth before evaporating into nothingness. 

Roger remained frozen on his knees, his forehead practically glued to the obsidian floor. The scent of ozone had been completely replaced by the thick, metallic stench of vaporized blood and burnt ozone. The entire confrontation had lasted less than a fraction of a millisecond. The strongest beings in fiction, the absolute pinnacle of their respective multiverses, had been slaughtered with less effort than a man required to draw breath. 

Zeus slowly turned his back to the raining gore, brushing a microscopic speck of ash from his pristine black tunic. He looked down at the kneeling maintenance worker, his expression shifting back into a mask of warm, friendly benevolence. “Roger,” Zeus said softly, his voice carrying the calm cadence of an old friend. 

Roger swallowed the bile rising in his throat, keeping his eyes firmly fixed on the polished tips of the king’s boots. “Yes, my King?” he whispered, his voice trembling so violently he could barely form the words. 

“You dropped your coffee,” Zeus noted, gesturing casually toward the shattered ceramic mug near Roger’s knee. “Clean up this mess, would you? And when you are finished, take the rest of the day off. The borderlands are quite secure.” 

Roger squeezed his eyes shut as a single drop of Superman’s boiling blood splattered against the stone near his thumb. “Right away, my King,” he choked out, completely paralyzed by the insurmountable, existential dread of existing in a reality governed by an unstoppable god. Zeus hummed a cheerful, ancient tune, turning his back on the empty void and leisurely walking back into the palace, leaving his infinitely loyal subject alone to sweep away the ashes of the greatest heroes who had ever lived.
