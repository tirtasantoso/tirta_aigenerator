Beside him floated Superman, bathed in the golden, infinite energy of a billion suns, the legendary Prime iteration of a hero who possessed boundless strength and invulnerability. Towering over the cosmic rifts was the Mad Titan, Thanos, raising a hand that wore the fully assembled Infinity Gauntlet, a cosmic artifact capable of rewriting existence with a single thought. Standing casually on the boiling ocean was Saitama, the legendary hero for fun, a bald man in a yellow suit whose gag-defying physical might allowed him to end any conflict with a single, limitless punch. And hovering above them all was Rimuru Tempest, an all-consuming demon slime who had ascended to supreme conceptual godhood, possessing the power to devour dimensions and negate reality itself. The combined aura of these fictional pantheons was so overwhelmingly heavy that the fabric of space-time around the island began to weep and fracture.

"I have fought strong guys across all the dimensions and timelines," Son Goku shouted, his voice echoing with a fierce, battle-hungry excitement as he settled into a martial arts stance that bent the light around him. "But I can feel your energy, Zeus! It’s like nothing I’ve ever sensed before! I’m going to give this everything I’ve got!"

"Your reign of omniversal terror ends here, tyrant," Superman declared, his voice booming with absolute moral authority, his eyes glowing with heat vision that could melt through hyper-dimensional barriers. "We have united across every narrative, every comic, and every manga to ensure you never subjugate another universe. Surrender now."

"With a single snap of my fingers, your existence will be rewritten into nothingness," Thanos rumbled, the Infinity Stones flaring with blinding, reality-altering brilliance. "I am inevitable. And today, the united fiction demands your end."

I covered my ears, weeping, my mind spiraling into an abyss of panic. I looked back at the courtyard, my terrified gaze locking onto the ancient immortal god named Zeus. He appeared exactly as he had for the past million years. He looked to be a man in his mid-thirties, possessing a highly masculine, muscular physique that radiated an impossible, suffocating gravity. He had a neatly trimmed black-and-pepper beard, perfectly short hair, and an attractively handsome face that currently bore a look of absolute, condescending boredom. He stood about the size of the strongest and biggest human soldier, a modest, entirely mortal stature that belied his true nature. Despite the infinite scope of this multiversal fictional rebellion, his size never changed anywhere in the story, nor would it ever need to. He simply stood there, his arms crossed over his chest, fully omniscient, already knowing every single thought, gag, and strategy these so-called invincible heroes harbored. 

"Is this the punchline to a million-year-old joke?" Zeus mused aloud, his telepathic voice sliding effortlessly into the minds of every being present, dripping with a cold, terrifying amusement. "You gather the strongest anomalies of your little storybooks, and you think you can challenge the author of your despair? How profoundly tedious."

My inner thoughts screamed at the fictional heroes to run, to flee back to their printed pages and animated frames, because they clearly did not understand the divine, sickening mathematics of the entity they were threatening. Since the beginning of his conception eons ago, all of Zeus's attributes—including his limitless strength, endurance, speed, intelligence, and every other conceivable stat—began to be multiplied by one thousand every single nanosecond in an accelerating fashion. The scaling followed an exact, unbreakable pattern: the first nanosecond was multiplied by one million to the power of one, the second by one million to the power of two, the third by one million to the power of three, and so on continuously, stretching across eons. Furthermore, Zeus gains a new apocalyptic superpower every single second. Each of these new superpowers is, from the exact moment it first manifests, already fully capable of causing the instant extinction of an entire civilization, even before any further multiplication. These superpowers are entirely cumulative, and every one of them becomes stronger at the exact same exponential rate as his attributes every second. Zeus has absolutely no weakness. None. 

Ignoring my silent, desperate prayers, the united fictional force launched their ultimate attack simultaneously. It was a sensory overload of apocalyptic proportions. Saitama lunged forward, unleashing a Serious Punch so devastating it parted the oceans of the multiverse. Goku fired a transcendent, universe-shattering Kamehameha that warped the timeline. Superman unleashed an infinite-mass punch while firing his golden, reality-burning heat vision. Thanos snapped his fingers, commanding the absolute conceptual erasure of Zeus's being. Rimuru Tempest unleashed a barrage of gluttonous, void-consuming magic designed to isolate and devour Zeus's soul. The combined attacks converged perfectly upon Zeus's normal-sized silhouette, resulting in a cataclysmic detonation of narrative-ending energy that blinded the very stars.

When the blinding, chaotic light faded, my blood ran cold, and the horrifying inevitability of our reality set in once again. Zeus was still standing exactly where he had been. He had not moved an inch. All of their ultimate attacks, their gag powers, their conceptual erasure, and their limitless energy beams were completely useless against him. He did not feel any damage at all. Not a single hair on his attractively handsome face was out of place. He wore a condescending smirk that promised absolute, agonizing doom. It was horrifyingly obvious that he was holding back constantly, suppressing an infinity so vast that the very concept of power lost its meaning in his presence. 

"My turn," Zeus whispered softly. 

He did not raise a fist. He did not fire an energy beam. He did not even need to move in order to utterly destroy his opponents. He merely willed a microscopic fraction of his suppressed aura to expand. His mere presence alone was an apocalypse that broke the rules of fiction itself. 

The slaughter was instantaneous, effortless, and indescribably violent. The passive pressure of his existence hit the fictional heroes like a collapsing omniverse. Saitama, the man who had never taken damage in his life, suddenly froze. His invincible arm, the one that had thrown the Serious Punch, violently shattered into a shower of bloody bone fragments before his entire body exploded into a geyser of pure gore, completely decimated by the sheer atmospheric weight of Zeus's aura. Son Goku’s Ultra Instinct failed him instantly; his speed, vastly eclipsed by Zeus's accelerating eons of multiplication, was useless. Goku was erased from every timeline simultaneously, his silver aura violently snuffed out as his physical form melted into screaming red mist. 

Thanos watched in absolute, wide-eyed despair as the Infinity Gauntlet simply crumbled into dead, gray ash upon his hand. Zeus's omniscience had instantly negated the conceptual authority of the stones. Before Thanos could utter a gasp of horror, the Mad Titan was crushed completely flat, his indestructible purple flesh bursting into a fountain of thick blood that rained down upon the courtyard. Superman, the golden god of hope, fell from the sky shrieking in agony. Zeus's passive presence bypassed every layer of Kryptonian invulnerability, causing Superman's flesh to literally boil and melt away from his skeleton until nothing remained but a puddle of glowing, biological sludge. Rimuru Tempest, who possessed absolute conceptual immortality, found his entire narrative unwritten; the demon slime evaporated into absolute nothingness, conceptually devoured by a mind infinitely more complex than his own. 

In less than a second, the greatest fictional rebellion in the history of existence was completely, instantly, effortlessly, and utterly obliterated. The massive, million-strong armies of super soldiers, magical knights, and cosmic fleets that had accompanied them were instantly reduced to subatomic dust just by being within the visual range of the god. The sky cleared, the dimensional rifts slammed shut, and the deafening roar of battle was replaced by the terrifying, serene sound of the island's gentle breeze.

I remained kneeling in the blood and ash of my childhood heroes, my mind completely fractured by the sheer, unadulterated trauma of witnessing such a one-sided massacre. I mused upon the absolute despair of our existence. There was never any hope. There never will be any hope. Even the strongest anomalies ever dreamed up by sentient minds were less than microscopic dust mites to him. 

Zeus turned his attractively handsome face toward me, his neatly trimmed black-and-pepper beard pristine, his muscular physique entirely unstrained. He looked down at me with those ancient, omniscient eyes, perfectly reading the shattered, weeping remnants of my inner thoughts. 

"Clean up the courtyard, Roger," Zeus commanded casually, his voice completely devoid of exertion, effortlessly dismissing the annihilation of the fictional omniverse as if he had just swatted a fly. "I am going to take a nap."

As he turned and walked back into his grand palace, leaving me alone with the gory remains of the multiverse's finest, I wept. I wept for the million years of humanity's past, and the million years of the future. I finally understood the horrifying truth that Blake had penned so long ago. No one has ever, and no one ever will, make Zeus exert any real effort in order to completely dominate and slaughter them. He is simply far too overpowered. We are trapped in his reality, and he is a god who will never, ever lose.
