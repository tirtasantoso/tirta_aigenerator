He fell to his knees, his vision filling with the sight of his own hands beginning to transmute into jagged shards of screaming glass. The pain was not physical; it was the sensation of his very soul being unmade by the King’s slight loss of interest.

I suppose I shouldn't let it bother me, Zeus said, his voice completely calm as he watched a guard’s ribcage crawl across the floor like a centipede. It’s just a nebula, after all. He waved a hand—a casual, nonchalant flick of his wrist as if shooing a fly. The reversal was so fast that John’s mind couldn't even process the transition. In the space between one heartbeat and the next, the guards were standing at attention again, their armor polished and their faces stoic. The blood on the walls vanished. The sun outside returned to its golden glory, the city reconstituted as if it had never trembled. The guard whose head had exploded was now adjusting his helmet, a slight, confused blink the only sign that he had just experienced the most violent death possible. The nebula in the hologram was now a perfect, flawless spiral of violet and gold, the black hole that had offended Zeus simply erased from the timeline of the universe.

John remained on his knees, his breath coming in ragged, terrified gasps. He looked at his hands; they were flesh again, but he could still feel the phantom sensation of being shattered glass. Zeus looked down at him, a small, kind smile playing on his lips. You’re shaking, John. Is the air too thin in here? I sometimes forget that even Blake’s line needs a bit of stability. John couldn't speak. His thoughts were a chaotic jumble of his father’s warnings, now realized as the most profound understatements in history. He had seen the truth: the King wasn't ruling the omniverse; the omniverse was a hallucination being held together by the King's whims. If Zeus ever truly got angry—not mildly disappointed, but genuinely furious—the concept of 'existence' would be nothing more than a footnote in a void that no longer had a name.

I... I apologize, my King, John stammered, his arrogance buried under a mountain of existential dread. I didn't realize... I didn't understand the scale. Zeus stood up, walking over to the young wizard with the easy grace of a predator that had forgotten it could kill. He reached out and patted John’s shoulder. The touch felt like the pressure of a million galaxies, yet it was intended to be gentle. Don't worry about it, John. It’s your first day. You’ll get used to the rhythm. Just keep things tidy, and we’ll have no problems. Blake was a good man because he knew how to tell a joke when the stars were dimming. I hope you have his wit. Zeus turned back to his throne, already looking bored again. John stood up on legs that felt like jelly, clutching his staff as if it were a life raft in an infinite ocean. He realized then that his father hadn't been warning him to keep Zeus happy for the sake of the empire; he had been warning him to keep Zeus happy because the King was the only thing keeping the void from noticing they were all there. As John backed away, bowing low, his internal monologue was a single, repeating prayer: Please, let the next ten thousand years be entirely, boringly, perfectly pleasant.
