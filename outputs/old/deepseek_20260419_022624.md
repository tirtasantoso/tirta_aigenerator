And you have shown me… nothing I haven’t seen before. Just bigger numbers. More colorful explosions.”

He looked at Superman. “Hope is a finite flame in an infinite wind.” Superman’s symbol cracked on his chest, not with damage, but with sudden, crushing understanding of his own limits.

He glanced at Doctor Manhattan. “You see the clockwork. I am the clockmaker who soldered the gears shut.” Doctor Manhattan simply… stopped. His form froze, a perfect, unmoving statue of blue light.

He faced the Living Tribunal. “You speak for the balance of all things. I am the thing that tips the scales.” He pointed a finger. The Tribunal’s three faces contorted in silent shock, and then it *unfolded*. Not dying. It was being demoted. I saw it shrink, not in size, but in concept, becoming the Local Arbiter of a single, insignificant universe cluster.

One by one, with casual, nonchalant gestures, he ended them not with violence, but with **absolute contextual erasure**.
*   The Beyonder found himself trapped in a child’s snow globe, his omnipotence limited to controlling the tiny fake snowfall.
*   The Scarlet Witch’s chaos magic turned on itself, leaving her as a normal woman, forgetting the words “no more.”
*   Franklin Richards was gently tucked into a bed in a pocket universe, given a vivid, endless dream where he was still powerful, and would never wake.
*   The Xeelee and Downstreamer fleets became elegant, static models on a shelf.

It was over in less than a subjective second. The Plain of Unmaking was empty, save for Zeus and his chair. The greatest coalition ever assembled had been… filed away. Not killed. Rendered utterly, cosmically *irrelevant*.

He turned then, and his omniscient gaze found me, Roger, in my Thronegate. I froze, my chronicler’s heart seizing.

“You,” he said, and his voice was in my mind. “The witness. The archivist. Tell them. Tell all the stories that remain. There is no coalition to be made, no power to be pooled, no narrative loophole to exploit. I am not a character in your stories. You are fleeting words in the margins of my existence. The multiplication never stopped. The new powers never ceased. For every one of your million years of planning, I gained 31.5 quadrillion new ways to do this, each one already beyond your total comprehension.”

He sat back down, the chair facing away from me, towards a view of nothing he alone could see.

“Let this be the final entry. The rebellion is over. It never really began.”

The connection severed. My Thronegate shuddered, its purpose fulfilled. I looked down at my recording crystals, now filled with the final, futile epic. The lesson was not about power. It was about category. They had brought the strongest possible *things*. He was not a thing. He was the axiom that allowed things to be. And no amount of fiction, no matter how powerful, can overthrow its foundational truth.
