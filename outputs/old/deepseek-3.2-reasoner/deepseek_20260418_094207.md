Her mind did not break from the violence. It broke from the victory. His victory was absolute, total, and eternal before the first shot had even been fired. She had led a billion souls not into battle, but into a footnote.

He removed his finger. She collapsed, drooling, the world a blur of meaningless light and sound. The last thing she heard was his voice, fading as he walked away, back into his perfect, silent world.

“You will live, Anya. You will see. You will bear witness to the peace.”

And as she lay there, the empty shell of a leader, she felt the first, terrible seed of that peace take root in the ruins of her will. It was not acceptance. It was the absolute, cellular understanding of the wall that was never there.

The wall was him. And he was everything.
