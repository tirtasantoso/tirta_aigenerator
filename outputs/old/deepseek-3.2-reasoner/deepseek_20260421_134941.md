He looked at me, and the ruthless cruelty bled away from his face, replaced by that warm, familiar affection. He saw my hands were steady, but my eyes were wide.

“Sorry about that, Tom,” he said, genuinely contrite. “Where were we?”

“The… the Silken Depths vintage,” I managed, my voice a little rough. “The one that tastes like a secret.”

He nodded, pleased. “Yes. Because the best secrets,” he said, taking a sip and gazing at the now-clear, peaceful sky where a few shimmering motes of divine ash were drifting down like ironic snow, “are the ones everyone knows, but nobody is foolish enough to speak.” He smiled, and for a moment, I could almost forget the symphony of horrors I had just witnessed. Almost. But the low, terrified hum of the sonic pollinator on the bench beside me was a permanent reminder.
