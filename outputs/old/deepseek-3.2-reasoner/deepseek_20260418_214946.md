**From the Personal Log of Roger, Thronegate Maintenance Technician, 3rd Class. Stardate: [Infinite Recurrence]**

It was my day to polish the Infinite Pillars in the Grand Atrium. A boring job, mostly. You wipe down a nebula, you buff a captive supernova to a nice shine. Keeps the place looking respectable. I was up on a floating scaffold, humming an old tune, when the fabric of the Grand Atrium… *sighed*.

Not a tear. Not a crack. A sigh of resignation from reality itself. And then, they were just… there. In the center of the Atrium, right in front of the Silent Throne where Uncle Zeus sometimes sits to think.

I nearly dropped my polisher. I’d seen a lot in my hundred and twenty years—conquered dimensions, peace treaties with abstract entities—but this was different. This was a committee of endings.

I recognized some from the Old Archives. There was **Superman**, from the Krypton-verse. Not the friendly one from the vids. This one’s eyes were like cold blue suns, his cape a swirl of spacetime, his body vibrating at a frequency that suggested he could shatter a universe by blinking. Beside him stood **Doctor Manhattan**, a being of pure quantum will, his azure form constantly deconstructing and reconstructing the local laws of physics just by existing. There was a Saiyan—**Son Goku**, I think—his hair a blazing corona of silver-white, energy crackling around him in a gravitational maelstrom, his very presence warping the Atrium’s perfect geometry.

But they were just the vanguard. Behind them stood a **Green Lantern** whose construct wasn’t a simple shape, but an entire, miniature Big Bang held in a fist. A being made of infinite **Infinity Stones**, each stone a pulsing heart of a different cosmic absolute. A lovecraftian horror that was all **Cthulhu**-esque angles and madness, but refined, focused, weaponized. A wizard in a pointy hat—**Merlin?**—who held a staff that was the crystallized concept of "Magic." And dozens, hundreds more, each the absolute pinnacle, the final boss, the unbeatable hero or unspeakable god of their entire narrative continuum.

They had united. They had crossed the void between stories. And they had come for one thing.

Superman spoke, his voice a vibration that made my bones hum. “Zeus. The Tyrant of the Omniverse. The consumer of narratives. We are the Final Alliance. We represent the concept of freedom, of growth, of endings that are not dictated by you. Your reign of silent conquest ends today.”

Doctor Manhattan’s voice was a chorus of probabilities. “We have analyzed your paradoxical nature. Your defense is predicated on the thought of a weakness. We have therefore purged all such thoughts from our collective consciousness through quantum certainty erasure. We approach you with no plan to exploit a flaw, only with the absolute, brute totality of our combined force. A force greater than infinity itself.”

Son Goku cracked his neck, a sound like collapsing stars. “Yeah! Let’s see how you handle this! I’ve been training for a million years in the Hyperbolic Time Chamber of All Realities!”

They weren’t lying. I could feel it. Their collective will was a singular, focused beam of “NO.” They didn’t think he had a weakness. They *knew* he didn’t. Their strategy was pure, overwhelming, existential force. It was, frankly, terrifying.

And then, Uncle Zeus was just… on his throne. He hadn’t arrived. He’d simply always been there, and we’d all just now noticed. He was leaning on one armrest, chin resting on his fist. He looked… mildly curious. Like a man watching an unusually complex anthill.

“I see,” Zeus said. His voice didn’t overpower theirs; it simply made theirs sound like echoes. “The strongest possible expressions from a billion fictional frames. An interesting confluence.”

“We have no weakness to give you, tyrant!” roared the Infinity Stone being. “We offer only total power!”

“I know,” Zeus said, almost kindly.

And then they attacked.

It was not a sequence of events. It was everything, everywhere, all at once, focused on a single point.
Superman unleashed his heat vision, but it wasn’t light or heat; it was the concentrated *idea of a sun*, given destructive intent.
Doctor Manhattan attempted to unmake the concept of “Zeus” from the quantum record.
Son Goku fired a Kamehameha wave that was the sum total of every ki attack ever thrown in every timeline of his universe.
The Green Lantern’s Big Bang construct was hurled.
The Infinity Stones activated in unison, aiming to rewrite Zeus’s very “story” to say “AND THEN HE DIED.”
Cthulhu’s horror invoked madness that could break the minds of galaxies.
Merlin spoke the First and Final Spell, the word that supposedly bound all magic to his will.

The Grand Atrium should have ceased to be. It should have become the ground zero of a new, chaotic meta-omniverse.

Nothing happened.

The attacks… converged. And then they just *stopped*. They didn’t hit a shield. They didn’t explode. They reached a point an inch from Zeus and attained a state of perfect, absolute zero. Not of temperature, but of consequence. All that infinite power, all that narrative weight, was rendered null. It was the equivalent of a child’s vivid drawing of a dragon being held up to a real hurricane; the paper simply ceased to matter.

Zeus hadn’t moved.

The silence was louder than the attack.

Doctor Manhattan was the first to understand. His blue form flickered. “No… It is not a defense against the thought of weakness. It is a defense against *any* thought of opposition. The moment we conceived of this alliance… the moment we defined ourselves in opposition to him… we triggered it. We have been feeding him. For a million years of our subjective planning time.”

Superman’s invincible frame seemed to sag. “Our certainty… our purity of purpose… it was just another form of belief. Another fuel.”

Son Goku stared at his hands, the silver aura guttering out. “My power… it’s not even registering. It’s like throwing a pebble at a mountain… but the mountain is the idea of mountains, and the pebble is less than a dream.”

Then Zeus spoke again. “Your analysis is partially correct. But you misunderstand the scale. You speak of ‘total power.’ Of ‘force greater than infinity.’” He finally moved, shifting slightly in his throne. “Those are concepts. I am not a concept. I am a fact. And my growth has been recursive for over one million, eleven thousand years. Your ‘infinite’ force is a static number. Mine is a function that has been multiplying forever. You have brought me the most interesting candle in all of creation. I am a supernova that has been exploding for an eon. You cannot overwhelm me. You cannot even interact with me, unless I permit it.”

He looked at them, not with anger, but with a profound, lonely finality. “You united on a premise: that your combined strength could challenge me. That premise was your first, last, and only mistake. The thought itself made your defeat an absolute, mathematical certainty before you took your first step across the void.”

He didn’t raise a hand. He didn’t summon a power. He simply… *finished the conversation*.

The Infinity Stone being looked at its gauntlet. The Stones weren’t just inert. They were *gone*. Not destroyed. Retconned from its personal history. It was just a being, now, empty and confused.
Merlin’s staff turned to sand, and the concept of “magic” as a force separate from Zeus’s will evaporated from his mind.
Cthulhu’s madness inverted, and it experienced a moment of terrifying, crystal-clear sanity before dissolving into a harmless, non-Euclidean mist.
The Green Lantern’ ring shattered, not with a sound, but with the quiet *pop* of a soap bubble.
Doctor Manhattan began to unravel, not into nothingness, but into everythingness at once, his consciousness dispersing into a harmless background hum of quantum foam.
Son Goku’s godly form reverted, not to a base state, but to that of a simple, mortal man, the memory of power fading like a dream.
Superman, last of all, met Zeus’s gaze. The hope, the defiance, the unshakable moral certainty in his eyes… flickered. And went out. Not with despair, but with the simple, crushing understanding of a fundamental law: *This is not a fight. This is the universe being.*

He didn’t die. None of them “died.” They were… *dismissed*. One moment they were there, the Final Alliance. The next, they were not. Not annihilated. Just *gone*, returned to their respective narratives, with the absolute, unbreakable meta-knowledge that there exists, above all stories, a Silent Throne, and on it, an Uncle who cannot be challenged.

The Grand Atrium was quiet again. The nebulae in the pillars swirled peacefully. I realized I’d been holding my breath.

From his throne, Zeus looked over at my floating scaffold. “Roger.”

My voice squeaked. “Y-yes, Uncle Zeus?”

“You missed a spot on the Pillar of Forbidden Galaxies. There’s a bit of cosmic dust in the lower left spiral.”

I looked. He was right. A barely perceptible smudge. “I… I’ll get right on it, sir.”

He gave a small, almost imperceptible nod, and then he was gone, leaving only the empty throne.

I picked up my polisher with trembling hands. My mind reeled. I’d just seen the combined might of every legend, every god, every unstoppable hero from a billion imaginations united and… *dismissed*. Not with a bang, not with a epic clash. With a quiet conversation and the enforcement of a paradox so absolute it made their infinite power look like a rounding error.

I applied the polish to the smudge, buffing it away. The nebula shone a bit brighter. The work is simple. The work is good. And somewhere, in a million different stories, Superman wonders why he sometimes wakes up with a vague, unshakable feeling of a throne room, and Goku trains just a little harder, chasing a shadow of a power he can’t even conceive of.

And here, in the quiet heart of everything, the polishing continues.
