I couldn't explain that the offer made me want to scream, not because it was bad, but because it was so utterly meaningless against the reality of his supremacy. He accepted my refusal with a gracious nod, utterly unbothered, as if my choice was as trivial as the wind.

He didn’t look at the weapon. He was amused, his gaze fixed on a distant point only he could see. He had deactivated his infinite shields. He had ordered Gent, his most pathetic, subservient aura, to withdraw. He had even willed a single, microscopic dead skin cell on his forearm to betray him, to weaken, to crack. It was theater. It was a joke played on the arrogance of existence.

He didn't just bring them back; he brought them back into a reality where they were permanently, irrevocably beneath him, their every movement now a testament to their own futility.

I am his court wizard, the descendant of his best friend, and as I looked into those infinite eyes, I felt my mind unravel. I tried to pray, but there was no god to hear me.
