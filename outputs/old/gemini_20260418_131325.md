The pressure sheared the skin, muscle, and fat from their skulls, leaving screaming, bloody bone before their craniums imploded under the atmospheric crush. 

The shockwave hit me. 

My femurs shattered. My tibias splintered into jagged shrapnel, tearing through the muscle of my calves and thighs. I was thrown backward, pinning beneath the twisted, crushing weight of the main tactical console. The pain was absolute, a blinding white flare that eradicated every thought in my brain save for one endless, agonizing scream. 

I lay there, choking on the copper tang of my own blood, my ruined legs turned to useless, bleeding paste. 

Zeus drifted through the empty space where the viewport used to be. His boots touched the bloody deck plating softly. He walked over the liquefied remains of my crew. He stood over me, looking down at my weeping, broken form. 

He didn't finish me. I saw it in his warm, terrible eyes. He needed me alive. He needed the architect of this slaughter to crawl back into the ashes of the old world and tell the survivors exactly what happened when they dared to challenge the patriarchy. 

I closed my eyes, weeping uncontrollably as the God-King turned his back on me, his pristine white tunic illuminated by the burning of my world. We had challenged the hierarchy. And in doing so, we had forged our own eternal chains.
