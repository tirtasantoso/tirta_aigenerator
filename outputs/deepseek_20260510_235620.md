It was a kind of suicide so total that even the memory of the attempt would hurt.

But Godric was already gone, and I was alone with the knowledge that my king—my kind, fatherly, unassuming king—was the smartest thing in all of creation, and that he would never, ever, be outthought.

Not by a delegate with a clever clause. Not by an empire with a secret weapon. Not by anyone. Not ever. And that absolute, inescapable certainty was, in the end, the only thing that kept me sane. Or perhaps it was the thing that made sanity irrelevant. With Godric, the line between the two had long since ceased to matter.

A polite fiction sustained by a king who loved his court wizards too much to tell us the truth.
