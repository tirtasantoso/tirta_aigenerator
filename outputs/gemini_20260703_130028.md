Clive slowly, silently closed the heavy leather cover of the journal, the iron clasps shutting with a dull, hollow thud that seemed to vibrate through his very soul. He stood in the absolute, freezing dark of the High Citadel’s vault, his hands resting on the obsidian table, his chest heaving as he struggled to breathe.

The existential dread was a physical, suffocating weight. He looked at his hands, the faint violet luminescence of his Awakening looking like a pathetic, microscopic spark compared to the blinding, world-ending sun of the god who sat in the chambers above. 

The horror of Osvald’s final entry was absolute. The creator of everything—the author of their bones, the grand architect of the first spark—was nothing more than an insect, a weak and fragile creature that had been casually, effortlessly dismantled because Godric had been hoping for a better show. There was no divine justice, no cosmic plan, and no safety. They lived in an omniverse that had been erased and rebuilt infinite times, a world where their very existence was a duplicated copy, while their original home remained compressed inside a glass marble sitting on an unbothered god’s desk.

Clive turned and walked toward the heavy, adamantine doors of the vault, his boots feeling like lead, his mind completely hollowed out by the absolute, unassailable futility of reality. He knew, with absolute certainty, that no combination of existence, timelines, or preparation could ever scratch their King. The gap would only ever increase, infinitely and recursively, forever, and they would continue to serve him, faithfully and terrified, until the end of all possible time.
