"Ah," Godric murmured, his voice a rich, comforting baritone that vibrated perfectly in the quiet garden. "The Author has arrived. The one who designed my mortal self, who drafted the stone of Olympus, and who... watched silently while Thronegate’s army slaughtered my wife, my daughters, and my best friend."

A soft, chilling smile broke across Godric’s ruggedly handsome face, his dark eyes crinkling with a playful, merciless satisfaction. He felt a sudden, mischievous whim. He wanted to see exactly what the Primordial Source was capable of. He wanted to know if the architect of all existence could even make his resting skin register the sensation of a strike.

"Sovereign-Absolute," Godric commanded, his voice flat and conversational. "Stand down. Gent, sleep. Concept-Erase Matrices, deactivate. All infinite recursive passive shields, collapse. Let the Creator have his turn."

The empty air around Godric shivered, a silent, subservient ripple as his evolved passive defense systems and infinite shields reluctantly dissolved into dormancy. For the first time in eons, the King stood completely naked to the cosmic wind, bare-chested and smiling, his heavily muscled forty-five-year-old body exposed to the blinding luminescence of the First Spark. He was using zero percent of his power and zero percent of his effort, keeping his absolute concealment perfectly sealed strictly to prevent his own resting presence from erasing the Creator before the strike could even land.

The Creator, seeing the shields fall, unleashed His ultimate, unmitigated strike. He did not fire magic or physical force; He fired the First Commandment. It was the absolute, raw power of the first second of existence—the foundational code that had birthed all energy, all matter, all souls, and all causality. It was a strike that bypassed the physical vessel entirely, designed to instantly, aggressively rewrite Godric’s conceptual identity, to reduce his existence to a state of non-being, to unwrite the very fact that he had ever been born. The power of the strike was so immense that the surrounding reality—decillions of multiverses, timelines, and spatial dimensions—was instantly, violently obliterated, turned into a silent, white-hot void of screaming primordial energy.

The First Commandment slammed directly into Godric’s face.

Absolute silence descended upon the void. The blinding light of the First Spark raged against his skin, a torrent of conceptual unmaking that should have erased the very memory of his existence from the fabric of time.

Godric did not move a single millimeter. He did not even blink. His neatly trimmed salt-and-pepper beard was not singed. His dark tunic was completely undamaged. The strike did not register as an impact. It did not register as a tickle. The absolute, unmitigated maximum output of the Primordial Source, the Creator of all things, was so pathetically small in relation to Godric’s resting, passive durability that the physical universe could not even resolve the contact. The strength and vitality of Godric’s body, recursively compounding by layered infinities every Planck second for eons, had reached a baseline so monstrously, ridiculously vast that the literal Creator of reality was less than a zero to him.

The roaring fire of the First Commandment slowly, pathetically sputtered and died, leaving the empty void quiet and cold.

The Creator, watching from His lightless height, experienced an immediate, crushing wave of absolute, mind-shattering existential dread. The shifting monolith of light violently shuddered, the perfect mathematical geometries of His form splintering and warping as the divine consciousness realized the terrifying, hopeless truth. The creature He had designed, the mortal king of Olympus He had allowed to suffer, had grown so far beyond His own primordial sovereignty that His ultimate, absolute best was completely, laughably meaningless. The Creator had become nothing more than a shivering insect standing in the shadow of an untouchable, unmaking god.

Godric let out a soft, amused chuckle, setting his teacup down on the stone table.

"How incredibly disappointing," Godric noted, his voice carrying the gentle, patronizing tone of a father watching a child fail to lift a small pebble. "Is that truly the absolute zenith of your power? The First Commandment? The great architect of the stars, reduced to this? You know, when I first emerged from stasis in Year Zero—back when I was so pathetically, laughably weak that I could only destroy the omniverse infinite times in zero seconds—I could have erased you with a half-asleep sigh. And yet, you come here today, after a googolplexian years of my amplification, and you expect to make me notice? How pathetically weak you are."

Godric did not stand up from his bench. He did not flex. He did not call upon his passive systems. He simply allowed an infinitesimally small fraction of his physical strength—effectively zero percent of his power, with zero percent effort—to express itself in the direction of the Creator.

The physical and conceptual deconstruction of the Primordial Source was instantaneous, agonizing, and grotesquely violent.

Because He was the Source, Godric’s strength forced Him to possess a physical, biological anatomy solely so He could experience the physical sensation of his own destruction.

I watched in raw, visceral horror as the newly formed, galaxy-spanning body of the Creator began to violently, recursively turn inside-out. His divine bones, forged from the first rules of physics, were instantaneously pulverized into a fine, subatomic paste by the sheer physical pressure of the shrug. His primordial blood, a substance that possessed the mass of decillions of collapsing universes, aggressively boiled and hissed as it was violently extruded from His pulverized pores. His eyes, spontaneously sprouting across His raw flesh, instantly liquefied under the blinding light of Godric's presence, running down His face in steaming pools of grease.

The Creator shrieked, a sound of absolute, mind-breaking agony that bypassed the eardrums and resonated directly within the marrow of my bones. He thrashed in the void, His long, slender limbs snapping and tearing from the bone as the physical force of Godric’s strength flayed His flesh in wet, screaming layers. Godric’s power did not allow Him to die quickly; his physical strength actively, passively held the Creator’s consciousness intact, forcing Him to remain completely conscious to register every single second of the excruciating, infinite torture.

The de-atomized, screaming mass of the Creator, along with the entire surrounding reality—every single multiverse, timeline, and dimension that had ever existed—was violently packed together. The raw physical mass of all possible existence was compressed inward by the sheer, unyielding weight of the kinetic feedback. It was a process of such dense, absolute gravitational pull that the entire canvas of reality was packed into a space smaller than a single quark.

Godric casually reached out his hand, pinching the sub-quark-sized speck of infinite mass between his thumb and index finger. Every planet, every star, and every life was placed exactly where it had been before the compression, their histories restored, their populations entirely healed.

Only the Creator remained unrestored, His character and His realm permanently deleted from the restored reality, leaving the rest of the omniverse entirely clean.

But the marble remained.

Clive slowly closed the journal, the heavy leather cover shutting with a soft, definitive thud that echoed like a hammer in the silent archive room. The entire remaining existence lived entirely at the mercy of an untouchable, unmaking god, and the very concept of reality was a lie. He closed his eyes, the cold, suffocating weight of the absolute futility settling forever into his soul.
