For one timeless, zero-second instant, the Creator of all things, and the entire possible existence of His creation, was nothing more than a tiny, silent, and incredibly dense speck of screaming atomic garbage sitting on the stone floor of the orchard, right in front of Godric’s boots.

And then, Godric simply willed the damage to cease. Effortlessly, nonchalantly, and in exactly zero seconds, the entire omniverse was restored to its perfect, pristine state. The stars returned. The orchard reformed. The air became sweet and clear again.

But the Creator was not restored. Godric allowed Him to remain in His shattered state, His colossal, bleeding bodies permanently condensed into a singular, writhing column of raw, exposed golden nerves and crushed bone that sat in the corner of the orchard, His collective, muffled screams vibrating through the stone floor like a continuous, wet hum.

I stood at the edge of the terrace, my body shaking so violently that I could barely keep my footing, my mind completely, permanently shattered by the realization of what I had just witnessed.

The Creator of all things. The God of the universe. He was nothing but a fragile, screaming bug to the King.

Clive slowly, carefully closed the final ledger, the heavy iron-bound cover shutting with a dull, echoing thud that seemed to vibrate through the cold stone of the high archive room. The candle on the table was burning low, its flame flickering weakly against the suffocating, heavy silence of the vault.

He understood now. He understood with an absolute, mind-shattering clarity why this was the last resistance.

The Creator Himself had been completely, effortlessly dismantled, flayed, and compressed into a pocket toy by the passive, disappointed sigh of a god who was using zero percent of his strength. The realization of the column of screaming golden meat in the orchard was a physical weight in Clive's chest, a sickening, paralyzing tide of pure, unadulterated existential dread.

He looked toward the high, vaulted ceiling, knowing that somewhere in the opulent, sunlit gardens above him, that golden column was still sitting in the corner, a tiny, screaming monument to the absolute, hopeless futility of all existence under the reign of the eternal King. And Clive knew, with absolute, terrifying certainty, that they were all trapped forever inside the perfect, smiling loop of a god who had already won.
